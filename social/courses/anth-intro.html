人类学导论

Introduction to Anthropology

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：无 | 学期：秋季

本课程是人类学专业的入门课程，全面介绍人类学的四大分支：文化人类学、生物人类学、考古学和语言人类学。学生将了解人类的生物进化与文化多样性，学习人类学的基本研究方法和核心概念，包括文化相对主义、民族志方法、亲属制度、宗教信仰等。课程通过丰富的民族志案例，引导学生以人类学的视角理解人类社会的复杂性和多样性。

This introductory course provides a comprehensive overview of anthropology's four subfields: cultural anthropology, biological anthropology, archaeology, and linguistic anthropology. Students explore human biological evolution and cultural diversity, learning fundamental research methods and core concepts including cultural relativism, ethnographic methods, kinship systems, and religious beliefs.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：人类学分支综合案例分析

选择一个具体社会文化现象，从人类学四大分支（文化、生物、考古、语言）的视角进行综合分析。结合各分支理论与方法，呈现人类社会复杂性的多维理解，撰写综合分析报告。

Choose a specific socio-cultural phenomenon and analyze it from the four anthropological subfields (cultural, biological, archaeological, linguistic). Integrate theories and methods from each subfield to present a multidimensional understanding, and write a comprehensive analysis report.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

📋 示例：做一个人类学观察研究，比如研究大学校园中的礼物交换文化。你需要在宿舍、社团、实验室等不同场景进行参与观察，记录礼物赠送的时机、类型和回礼规则，访谈10名同学了解他们的送礼观念，用莫斯礼物理论进行分析。
步骤 1
现象选择与分支视角梳理
本步骤的核心任务是选择一个具有多维分析潜力的社会文化现象，并系统梳理人类学四大分支的理论视角。选题质量直接决定后续分析的深度与广度，需要确保现象本身具有文化、生物、历史、语言等多重维度可供挖掘。通过初步文献检索，验证各分支视角的适用性与资料可得性。

• 从日常生活或媒体报道中筛选3-5个候选现象，运用人类学想象力评估其文化、生物、考古、语言四个维度的分析潜力，最终确定1个核心研究对象
• 梳理四大分支的核心理论框架：文化人类学的文化相对主义与深描、生物人类学的进化论与适应、考古学的物质文化分析、语言人类学的话语实践理论
• 为每个分支初步确定2-3个核心概念或理论工具，撰写500字的选题论证报告，说明各视角的切入路径与预期分析价值
产出：选题论证报告（含现象描述、四大分支视角映射图、核心概念清单、初步参考文献10篇）｜ 质量标准：选题具有多维分析空间，四大分支视角映射清晰合理，每个视角均有明确的理论工具支撑
步骤 2
分支文献资料系统收集
本步骤的核心任务是针对四大分支分别进行深度文献检索与资料收集，建立各分支的理论基础与案例库。文献收集需要兼顾经典理论著作与当代实证研究，确保分析既有理论深度又有经验支撑。使用文献管理软件对资料进行分类整理，为后续分析做好准备。

• 使用JSTOR、Project MUSE、AnthroSource等数据库，按分支检索关键词，每个分支收集至少15篇核心文献，其中经典著作3-5部、近十年实证研究10篇以上
• 制作文献摘要表，每篇文献记录核心观点、研究方法、关键发现与可借鉴之处，按分支分类归档，总字数不少于3000字
• 收集与研究现象相关的民族志案例、考古报告、生物数据和语料资料，建立多模态资料文件夹，确保每个分支至少有2个具体案例可资分析
产出：文献资料库（含Zotero分类文献库、文献摘要表、多模态案例资料集、分支文献综述各500字）｜ 质量标准：文献覆盖经典与前沿，每个分支不少于15篇核心文献，资料类型多样且与研究对象高度相关
步骤 3
四大分支多维深度分析
本步骤的核心任务是运用各分支的理论与方法，对选定现象进行独立而深入的分析。每个分支的分析都需要遵循该学科的研究规范与思维方式，展现不同分支视角下现象呈现出的不同面向。分析过程中要注重理论与经验材料的结合，避免空泛的理论套用。

• 文化人类学分析：运用民族志方法和深描理论，分析现象的文化意义、符号体系、价值观念与社会实践，撰写2000字的文化分析章节
• 生物人类学分析：从进化心理学、人类适应、生物-文化交互作用等角度，分析现象的生物学基础与文化建构的互动关系，撰写1500字的生物分析章节
• 考古学分析：通过物质文化遗存、长时段变迁、文明兴衰等视角分析现象的历史深度，语言人类学分析：从话语实践、语言意识形态、语言与身份等角度分析现象的语言维度，各撰写1500字
产出：四章分支分析文稿（每章1500-2000字，含理论框架、案例分析、小结，共约7000字）｜ 质量标准：每个分支分析都有明确的理论框架和具体的经验证据支撑，分析逻辑严谨，展现各分支独特的学术视角
步骤 4
跨分支综合整合与理论创新
本步骤的核心任务是将四大分支的分析进行有机整合，形成对现象的整体性、立体化理解。综合不是简单的拼接，而是要找到各分支视角之间的内在关联与互补之处，揭示单一视角无法看到的深层结构与动态机制。通过对话与碰撞，可能产生新的理论洞见。

• 制作分支视角关联图，标注四大分支分析之间的理论衔接点、互补关系与潜在矛盾，识别出3-5个关键整合点
• 从生物-文化协同进化、物质文化-话语实践互动、结构-能动性辩证关系等元理论层面，对各分支分析进行综合阐释，撰写2500字的综合分析章节
• 反思四大分支各自的局限与盲区，讨论整合视角的方法论意义，提出1-2个具有创新性的综合分析命题或研究假设
产出：综合分析章节（含分支关联图、整合分析正文2500字、理论创新点、方法论反思）｜ 质量标准：整合分析超越简单拼接，揭示多维度间的内在关联，有明确的理论创新点，展现整体性学术视野
步骤 5
报告撰写、规范引用与学术打磨
本步骤的核心任务是将各部分分析整合成一篇结构完整、逻辑严密、语言规范的学术报告。报告需要遵循学术写作规范，包括摘要、引言、主体各章、结论、参考文献等完整结构。引用格式需统一采用芝加哥注释格式或APA格式，确保学术严谨性。

• 撰写300字中英文摘要、500字引言（含研究问题、研究意义、分析框架）和800字结论（含主要发现、研究局限、未来研究方向），与之前的分析章节整合为完整报告
• 统一引用格式（芝加哥作者-日期格式或APA第7版），确保正文内所有观点都有文献支撑，参考文献列表不少于40篇，格式完全规范
• 进行三轮修改：第一轮调整结构与逻辑，第二轮润色语言与表达，第三轮校对格式与引文，最终形成不少于12000字的完整报告
产出：完整的综合分析报告（约12000字，含摘要、引言、四章分支分析、综合分析、结论、参考文献，格式规范）｜ 质量标准：报告结构完整、逻辑清晰、论证充分、引用规范，达到本科课程优秀结课论文水平

Steps

Step 1
Phenomenon Selection and Subfield Mapping
The core task of this step is to select a socio-cultural phenomenon with multi-dimensional analytical potential and systematically map the theoretical perspectives of anthropology's four subfields. The quality of topic selection directly determines the depth and breadth of subsequent analysis, ensuring the phenomenon has cultural, biological, historical, and linguistic dimensions worth exploring. Preliminary literature search verifies the applicability and data availability of each subfield perspective.

• Screen 3-5 candidate phenomena from daily life or media, evaluate analytical potential across cultural, biological, archaeological, and linguistic dimensions using anthropological imagination, and finalize 1 core research subject
• Map core theoretical frameworks of the four subfields: cultural relativism and thick description in cultural anthropology, evolution and adaptation in biological anthropology, material culture analysis in archaeology, and discourse practice theory in linguistic anthropology
• Identify 2-3 core concepts or theoretical tools for each subfield, write a 500-word topic proposal explaining analytical approaches and expected value of each perspective
Deliverable: Topic proposal (including phenomenon description, four-subfield perspective mapping, core concept list, 10 preliminary references) | Quality standard: Topic has multi-dimensional analytical space, four-subfield perspective mapping is clear and reasonable, each perspective is supported by explicit theoretical tools
Step 2
Systematic Subfield Literature Collection
The core task of this step is to conduct in-depth literature search and data collection for each of the four subfields, establishing theoretical foundations and case libraries. Literature collection should balance classic theoretical works and contemporary empirical research, ensuring both theoretical depth and empirical support. Literature management software is used to organize materials for subsequent analysis.

• Use databases such as JSTOR, Project MUSE, and AnthroSource to search by keywords per subfield, collect at least 15 core articles per subfield including 3-5 classic works and 10+ recent empirical studies
• Create literature summary tables, recording core arguments, research methods, key findings, and insights for each article, organized by subfield, totaling no less than 3000 words
• Collect ethnographic cases, archaeological reports, biological data, and linguistic materials related to the research phenomenon, build a multimodal materials folder, ensuring at least 2 specific cases per subfield
Deliverable: Literature database (including Zotero categorized library, literature summary tables, multimodal case collection, 500-word subfield literature reviews each) | Quality standard: Literature covers classics and frontiers, no less than 15 core articles per subfield, diverse data types highly relevant to research subject
Step 3
In-depth Multi-subfield Analysis
The core task of this step is to conduct independent and in-depth analysis of the selected phenomenon using theories and methods from each subfield. Each subfield's analysis must follow the disciplinary research norms and ways of thinking, revealing different facets of the phenomenon from various perspectives. The analysis should emphasize integration of theory and empirical materials, avoiding empty theoretical application.

• Cultural anthropology analysis: Apply ethnographic methods and thick description theory to analyze cultural meaning, symbolic systems, values, and social practices of the phenomenon, write a 2000-word cultural analysis chapter
• Biological anthropology analysis: Analyze interaction between biological foundations and cultural construction from perspectives of evolutionary psychology, human adaptation, and bio-cultural interaction, write a 1500-word biological analysis chapter
• Archaeological analysis: Explore historical depth through material culture remains, long-term change, and civilization rise/fall; Linguistic anthropology analysis: Examine linguistic dimension through discourse practice, language ideology, and language and identity, write 1500 words each
Deliverable: Four subfield analysis chapters (1500-2000 words each, including theoretical framework, case analysis, summary, totaling approximately 7000 words) | Quality standard: Each subfield analysis has clear theoretical framework and specific empirical evidence, rigorous analytical logic, demonstrating unique academic perspectives of each subfield
Step 4
Cross-subfield Integration and Theoretical Innovation
The core task of this step is to organically integrate the analyses of the four subfields to form a holistic, three-dimensional understanding of the phenomenon. Integration is not simple juxtaposition but finding internal connections and complementarities between subfield perspectives, revealing deep structures and dynamic mechanisms invisible from single perspectives. New theoretical insights may emerge through dialogue and engagement.

• Create subfield perspective connection map, annotate theoretical junctions, complementary relationships, and potential contradictions among the four subfield analyses, identifying 3-5 key integration points
• Conduct comprehensive interpretation from meta-theoretical levels such as bio-cultural coevolution, material culture-discourse practice interaction, and structure-agency dialectics, write a 2500-word synthesis chapter
• Reflect on limitations and blind spots of each subfield, discuss methodological significance of integrated perspective, propose 1-2 innovative synthetic analytical propositions or research hypotheses
Deliverable: Synthesis chapter (including subfield connection map, 2500-word synthesis text, theoretical innovations, methodological reflections) | Quality standard: Synthesis transcends simple juxtaposition, reveals internal connections among multiple dimensions, has clear theoretical innovations, demonstrates holistic academic vision
Step 5
Report Writing, Citation Formatting and Academic Polishing
The core task of this step is to integrate all analytical components into a structurally complete, logically rigorous, and stylistically standardized academic report. The report must follow academic writing conventions, including abstract, introduction, body chapters, conclusion, and references. Citation format should uniformly follow Chicago Notes format or APA format to ensure academic rigor.

• Write 300-word Chinese and English abstracts, 500-word introduction (including research questions, significance, analytical framework), and 800-word conclusion (including main findings, limitations, future directions), integrate with previous analysis chapters into complete report
• Unify citation format (Chicago author-date or APA 7th edition), ensure all claims in text have literature support, reference list of no fewer than 40 entries with fully standardized format
• Conduct three rounds of revision: first round adjusts structure and logic, second polishes language and expression, third proofreads format and citations, resulting in complete report of no less than 12,000 words
Deliverable: Complete comprehensive analysis report (approximately 12,000 words, including abstract, introduction, four subfield analyses, synthesis, conclusion, references, properly formatted) | Quality standard: Report has complete structure, clear logic, sufficient argumentation, proper citations, reaching excellent undergraduate course paper standard
