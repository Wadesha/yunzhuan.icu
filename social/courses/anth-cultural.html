文化人类学

Cultural Anthropology

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：人类学导论 | 学期：春季

本课程深入探讨文化人类学的核心议题，包括亲属制度、经济人类学、政治人类学、宗教人类学、性别与性等领域。学生将通过阅读经典民族志作品，了解不同文化的社会组织方式、价值体系和生活实践。课程强调文化相对主义视角，培养学生对跨文化差异的敏感度和分析能力，以及运用民族志方法研究当代社会问题的能力。

This course delves into core topics of cultural anthropology including kinship, economic anthropology, political anthropology, religion, gender, and sexuality. Students read classic ethnographies to understand social organization, value systems, and life practices across cultures. The course emphasizes cultural relativism, developing cross-cultural sensitivity and analytical skills for applying ethnographic methods to contemporary social issues.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：文化制度民族志比较研究

选择某一文化制度（亲属、经济、政治、宗教或性别），通过阅读民族志文献进行跨文化比较分析。运用人类学理论阐释不同文化中的制度形态与社会意义，撰写比较研究论文。

Choose a cultural institution (kinship, economy, politics, religion, or gender) and conduct cross-cultural comparison through ethnographic literature. Apply anthropological theory to interpret institutional forms and social meanings across cultures, and write a comparative research paper.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

📋 示例：做一个文化人类学分析——比如研究某个文化习俗的意义，写出分析报告。
步骤 1
田野点选择与研究问题凝练
本步骤的核心任务是确定具体的田野调查地点，并基于初步观察凝练出有学术价值的研究问题。田野点的选择需要兼顾可达性、研究价值和伦理可行性，研究问题要具体、聚焦且具有理论对话空间。通过探索性走访和背景资料收集，为正式田野工作奠定基础。

• 在导师指导下筛选2-3个候选田野点，从研究主题契合度、进入可行性、社区规模与异质性等维度进行评估，最终确定1个主田野点
• 进行3-5天的探索性走访，参与社区日常活动，完成2万字以上的初步田野笔记，从中提炼出3-5个候选研究问题
• 结合文化人类学核心理论（文化相对主义、深描、实践理论等），将研究问题聚焦为1个核心问题和2-3个子问题，撰写1500字的研究设计初稿
产出：研究设计初稿（含田野点背景介绍、核心研究问题、子问题树、理论框架初步构想、伦理考量清单）｜ 质量标准：研究问题具体聚焦，具有明确的理论对话对象，田野点选择合理且可行，伦理考量周全
步骤 2
田野进入与关系建立
本步骤的核心任务是顺利进入田野现场，与研究对象建立信任关系，为后续深度参与和访谈铺平道路。田野进入是文化人类学研究的关键环节，需要处理好"局内人-局外人"的辩证关系，既保持足够的融入以获取真实信息，又维持必要的距离以保证分析的客观性。

• 通过正式或非正式渠道联系关键信息人（key informant），准备详细的自我介绍与研究说明，以谦逊的态度获得社区认可，完成至少3位核心信息人的初步接触
• 采用"慢慢进来"策略，前两周以旁观者和学习者的身份参与社区活动，每天撰写详细的田野笔记（不少于3000字/天），记录自己的进入过程与感受
• 主动参与社区劳动或公共活动，通过实际行动建立互信关系，在第二周末之前获得至少1位社区权威人士的明确支持，并确认5位以上愿意接受深度访谈的对象
产出：田野进入记录（含田野日记4万字以上、关键信息人档案、关系网络图、伦理审查确认书）｜ 质量标准：成功进入田野并建立基本信任关系，田野笔记详实系统，伦理规范得到遵守
步骤 3
参与观察与深度访谈
本步骤是田野调查的核心阶段，通过参与式观察和深度访谈收集第一手民族志资料。参与观察要求长期浸泡在社区生活中，记录日常实践的细节；深度访谈则通过半结构化对话挖掘当地人的意义世界与主观体验。两种方法相互补充，共同构建厚实的民族志描述。

• 开展不少于4周的全职参与观察，覆盖工作日与周末、日常与仪式等不同场景，每天撰写3000字以上的田野笔记，总笔记量不少于25万字，使用Geertz的深描方法记录细节
• 设计半结构化访谈提纲，包含3个核心维度、15-20个主要问题，对15-20位信息人进行深度访谈，每人访谈1-3次，每次60-90分钟，全程录音并逐字转录，转录文本不少于15万字
• 收集物质文化资料（照片、实物、文档等），建立田野资料档案库，按主题（亲属制度、经济活动、宗教仪式、政治组织等）进行分类编码，形成初步的编码体系
产出：田野资料集（含田野笔记25万字以上、访谈转录文本15万字以上、照片200张以上、物质文化资料档案、初步编码手册）｜ 质量标准：资料收集系统全面，覆盖社区生活的主要维度，访谈样本具有代表性，田野笔记达到深描要求
步骤 4
民族志编码与理论分析
本步骤的核心任务是对田野资料进行系统编码和理论分析，从经验材料中提炼出具有学术价值的分析框架与核心论点。编码过程遵循"自下而上"的扎根理论路径，同时保持与既有理论的对话。通过开放式编码、主轴编码和选择性编码的三级编码程序，逐步构建理论解释。

• 使用NVivo或Atlas.ti质性分析软件，对田野笔记和访谈转录进行三级编码：开放式编码产生50个以上初始概念，主轴编码归为10-15个核心范畴，选择性编码提炼出1个核心范畴和故事线
• 选取文化人类学的2-3个核心理论（如布迪厄的实践理论、格尔茨的文化阐释、特纳的仪式理论等）作为对话框架，将编码结果与理论进行对照与互释
• 构建民族志叙事框架，确定3-4个核心分析章节的主题与论点，撰写每章的详细大纲，确保经验材料与理论分析有机融合
产出：编码分析报告（含三级编码体系、理论对话备忘录、章节大纲、核心论点提炼）｜ 质量标准：编码系统严谨，理论与经验材料结合紧密，核心论点有充分的民族志证据支撑
步骤 5
民族志撰写与学术规范
本步骤的核心任务是将分析结果撰写为一篇规范的民族志研究报告。文化人类学写作强调"深描"与"厚叙述"，既要呈现当地人的视角，又要保持学术分析的深度。报告需要遵循学术写作规范，包括反思性声明，说明研究者的身份、立场对研究过程的影响。

• 按照标准民族志结构撰写：引言（含研究问题与方法）、田野点背景、3-4章主体分析、结论、参考文献、附录（访谈提纲、编码手册等），全文不少于15000字
• 在写作中贯穿反思性：明确说明研究者的身份定位、田野进入过程、与信息人的关系、以及这些因素如何影响资料的收集与解读，撰写1000字以上的方法论反思
• 进行三轮修改完善：第一轮调整结构与论证逻辑，第二轮润色语言（确保民族志叙述生动且学术严谨），第三轮校对格式与引文，统一使用芝加哥注释格式，参考文献不少于30篇
产出：完整的民族志研究报告（约15000字，含反思性方法论说明、深描式叙事、理论分析、规范引文）｜ 质量标准：民族志描述厚实生动，理论分析深入，反思意识明确，学术规范严谨，达到本科民族志课程优秀水平

Steps

Step 1
Field Site Selection and Research Question Formulation
The core task of this step is to determine the specific fieldwork site and formulate academically valuable research questions based on preliminary observation. Field site selection must balance accessibility, research value, and ethical feasibility; research questions must be specific, focused, and have theoretical dialogue space. Exploratory visits and background data collection lay the foundation for formal fieldwork.

• Screen 2-3 candidate field sites under advisor guidance, evaluate dimensions including research topic fit, entry feasibility, community size and heterogeneity, and finalize 1 main field site
• Conduct 3-5 days of exploratory visits, participate in daily community activities, complete over 20,000 words of preliminary field notes, and extract 3-5 candidate research questions
• Integrate core cultural anthropology theories (cultural relativism, thick description, practice theory), focus research questions into 1 core question and 2-3 sub-questions, write 1500-word preliminary research design
Deliverable: Preliminary research design (including field site background, core research questions, sub-question tree, initial theoretical framework, ethical considerations checklist) | Quality standard: Research questions are specific and focused, have clear theoretical dialogue partners, field site selection is reasonable and feasible, ethical considerations are comprehensive
Step 2
Field Entry and Rapport Building
The core task of this step is to successfully enter the field site and build trusting relationships with research subjects, paving the way for subsequent deep participation and interviews. Field entry is a critical step in cultural anthropology research, requiring handling of the insider-outsider dialectic—maintaining sufficient integration to obtain authentic information while preserving necessary distance for analytical objectivity.

• Contact key informants through formal or informal channels, prepare detailed self-introduction and research explanation, gain community recognition with humble attitude, complete initial contact with at least 3 core informants
• Adopt "slow entry" strategy, participate in community activities as observer and learner for first two weeks, write detailed field notes daily (no less than 3000 words/day), record entry process and personal reflections
• Actively participate in community labor or public activities, build mutual trust through practical action, obtain explicit support from at least 1 community authority figure by end of second week, and confirm 5+ interviewees willing to conduct in-depth interviews
Deliverable: Field entry records (including over 40,000 words of field diaries, key informant profiles, relationship network map, ethics review confirmation) | Quality standard: Successfully entered field and established basic trust relationships, field notes are detailed and systematic, ethical norms are observed
Step 3
Participant Observation and In-depth Interview
This step is the core phase of fieldwork, collecting first-hand ethnographic data through participant observation and in-depth interviews. Participant observation requires long-term immersion in community life, recording details of daily practice; in-depth interviews explore local meaning worlds and subjective experiences through semi-structured dialogue. The two methods complement each other to build thick ethnographic description.

• Conduct no less than 4 weeks of full-time participant observation, covering weekdays and weekends, daily and ritual scenes, write over 3000 words of field notes daily, total notes no less than 250,000 words, using Geertz's thick description method
• Design semi-structured interview guide with 3 core dimensions and 15-20 main questions, conduct in-depth interviews with 15-20 informants, 1-3 sessions per person, 60-90 minutes each, full recording and verbatim transcription totaling no less than 150,000 words
• Collect material culture data (photos, artifacts, documents), build field data archive, code by theme (kinship, economic activity, religious ritual, political organization), form initial coding system
Deliverable: Field dataset (including over 250,000 words of field notes, over 150,000 words of interview transcripts, 200+ photos, material culture archive, initial coding manual) | Quality standard: Data collection is systematic and comprehensive, covering major dimensions of community life, interview sample is representative, field notes meet thick description requirements
Step 4
Ethnographic Coding and Theoretical Analysis
The core task of this step is to conduct systematic coding and theoretical analysis of field data, extracting academically valuable analytical frameworks and core arguments from empirical materials. The coding process follows a "bottom-up" grounded theory approach while maintaining dialogue with existing theories. Theoretical explanation is gradually constructed through three-level coding: open coding, axial coding, and selective coding.

• Use NVivo or Atlas.ti qualitative analysis software for three-level coding of field notes and interview transcripts: open coding generates 50+ initial concepts, axial coding organizes into 10-15 core categories, selective coding extracts 1 core category and storyline
• Select 2-3 core cultural anthropology theories (e.g., Bourdieu's practice theory, Geertz's cultural interpretation, Turner's ritual theory) as dialogue framework, compare and mutually interpret coding results with theory
• Construct ethnographic narrative framework, determine themes and arguments for 3-4 core analytical chapters, write detailed outlines for each chapter, ensuring organic integration of empirical materials and theoretical analysis
Deliverable: Coding analysis report (including three-level coding system, theoretical dialogue memos, chapter outlines, core argument extraction) | Quality standard: Coding system is rigorous, theory and empirical materials are closely integrated, core arguments are supported by sufficient ethnographic evidence
Step 5
Ethnographic Writing and Academic Standards
The core task of this step is to write the analytical results into a规范 ethnographic research report. Cultural anthropological writing emphasizes "thick description" and "thick narrative," presenting the native perspective while maintaining depth of academic analysis. The report must follow academic writing conventions, including reflexive statement explaining how the researcher's identity and positionality influenced the research process.

• Write according to standard ethnographic structure: introduction (research questions and methods), field site background, 3-4 body analysis chapters, conclusion, references, appendices (interview guide, coding manual), total text no less than 15,000 words
• Incorporate reflexivity throughout writing: explicitly state researcher's positionality, field entry process, relationships with informants, and how these factors influenced data collection and interpretation, write over 1000 words of methodological reflection
• Conduct three rounds of revision: first round adjusts structure and argumentative logic, second polishes language (ensuring ethnographic narrative is vivid yet academically rigorous), third proofreads format and citations, uniformly using Chicago Notes format, no fewer than 30 references
Deliverable: Complete ethnographic research report (approximately 15,000 words, including reflexive methodology statement, thick descriptive narrative, theoretical analysis,规范 citations) | Quality standard: Ethnographic description is thick and vivid, theoretical analysis is in-depth, reflexive awareness is clear, academic standards are rigorous, reaching excellent undergraduate ethnography course level
