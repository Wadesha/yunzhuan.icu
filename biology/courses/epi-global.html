全球卫生

Global Health

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：流行病学、卫生政策 | 学期：第四学期

本课程从全球视角审视健康问题及其应对策略。内容涵盖全球疾病负担与健康不平等、全球卫生治理体系（WHO、世界银行、非政府组织等）、传染病与非传染性疾病的全球防控、妇幼健康与营养、全球卫生安全与国际卫生条例、卫生援助与国际合作、全球健康公平的社会决定因素。通过案例研究和小组项目，培养学生的全球健康视野和跨文化协作能力。

This course examines health issues and response strategies from a global perspective. Topics include global burden of disease and health inequalities, global health governance systems (WHO, World Bank, NGOs, etc.), global prevention and control of infectious and non-communicable diseases, maternal and child health and nutrition, global health security and international health regulations, health aid and international cooperation, and social determinants of global health equity.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：全球卫生议题案例研究与政策建议

选择一个全球卫生议题（如传染病防控、妇幼健康或卫生不平等），分析其现状、挑战和应对策略，提出政策建议。撰写案例分析报告。

Select a global health issue (such as infectious disease control, maternal and child health, or health inequality), analyze its current status, challenges, and response strategies, and propose policy recommendations. Write a case analysis report.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

示例：关注一个全球健康议题——比如传染病跨境传播，分析防控策略。
步骤 1
全球卫生问题选择与研究设计
本步骤的核心任务是选择一个重要的全球卫生问题，设计跨国/跨区域的流行病学研究方案。需要从全球疾病负担、健康公平、传染病防控、慢性非传染性疾病等全球卫生热点领域选择研究主题，考虑不同国家/地区的社会经济、文化、卫生体系差异。全球卫生研究需要国际视野和跨文化视角。

• 选择全球卫生主题：从全球疾病负担（GBD）研究、千年发展目标/可持续发展目标（MDG/SDG）相关健康议题、新发再发传染病、跨国健康公平、全球卫生安全等中选择研究主题
• 研究设计：确定研究类型（多国横断面研究、跨国队列研究、国际多中心临床试验、生态性研究），选择研究国家/地区（考虑不同收入水平、不同区域、不同卫生体系），确定比较框架
• 伦理与合作：考虑国际研究伦理（WHO伦理指南、不同国家IRB审查要求），建立国际合作机制，明确数据共享协议，尊重当地文化和社区参与
产出：全球卫生研究设计方案（含研究背景、研究问题、研究设计、国家选择、伦理框架、合作机制）｜ 质量标准：研究主题具有全球意义，设计考虑跨国差异，伦理框架完善，合作机制可行
步骤 2
全球数据收集与标准化
本步骤的核心任务是系统收集全球/多国数据，确保数据的标准化和可比性。全球卫生研究的数据来源多样，包括国际组织数据库、各国监测系统、现场调查等，数据质量和定义的差异是主要挑战。数据标准化是跨国比较研究的前提和基础。

• 数据来源确定：确定数据来源——WHO/UNICEF/世界银行等国际组织数据库、全球疾病负担（GBD）数据库、各国卫生统计年鉴、多国合作调查（如DHS、STEPwise）、发表文献系统综述
• 数据标准化：统一疾病定义和诊断标准（ICD编码、WHO标准），统一测量单位和方法，进行货币购买力平价（PPP）转换，年龄/性别结构标准化（直接法/间接法）
• 数据质量评估：评估各国数据质量（完整性、可靠性、时效性），识别数据缺失和异常，建立数据质量评级体系，记录数据局限性和不确定性
产出：全球卫生数据集（含多国标准化数据、数据字典、质量评估报告、标准化方法说明）｜ 质量标准：数据来源权威可靠，标准化方法科学合理，数据质量评估全面，可比性强
步骤 3
全球疾病负担与健康公平分析
本步骤的核心任务是分析全球/区域疾病负担，评估健康公平状况。需要计算疾病负担指标（DALY、YLL、YLD），分析健康状况的社会决定因素，评估不同人群、不同国家间的健康公平性。疾病负担和健康公平分析是制定全球卫生政策的重要依据。

• 疾病负担分析：计算全球/区域/国家层面的发病率、患病率、死亡率、伤残调整寿命年（DALY）、过早死亡损失寿命年（YLL）、伤残损失寿命年（YLD），分析疾病谱变化和流行病学转型
• 健康公平分析：按收入水平、性别、教育、居住地等社会经济维度分解健康指标差异，计算健康不公平指数（基尼系数、集中指数、斜率指数），分析社会决定因素对健康的影响
• 趋势与预测：分析过去20-30年全球健康变化趋势，使用时间序列模型和人口预测模型预测未来疾病负担，评估SDG目标达成进度
产出：全球疾病负担与健康公平分析报告（含疾病负担估算、不公平指数、趋势预测、地区比较）｜ 质量标准：疾病负担估算方法规范，公平分析维度全面，趋势预测科学合理
步骤 4
全球卫生干预效果评价与政策分析
本步骤的核心任务是评价全球卫生干预措施的效果，分析卫生政策的影响。需要系统评估疫苗接种、疾病控制项目、卫生体系改革等干预措施在不同国家的实施效果和成本效益，分析政策实施的促进因素和障碍。干预评价为全球卫生资源配置提供决策依据。

• 干预效果评价：选择重点干预措施（疫苗接种、抗病毒治疗、控烟、食盐加碘、母婴健康项目等），使用meta分析、系统综述、准实验研究设计评价干预效果，计算需治疗人数（NNT）、疫苗有效性（VE）等指标
• 成本效益分析：进行成本-效果分析（CEA）、成本-效用分析（CUA）、成本-效益分析（CBA），计算增量成本效果比（ICER），评估干预措施的可负担性和可持续性
• 政策分析：分析不同国家卫生政策的实施效果，识别成功因素和失败教训，比较不同卫生体系模式的绩效（WHO卫生系统框架：服务提供、人力、信息、资金、领导力），提出政策建议
产出：全球卫生干预评价与政策分析报告（含干预效果证据、成本效益分析、政策比较、建议）｜ 质量标准：干预评价证据充分，成本效益分析规范，政策分析深入有洞见
步骤 5
全球卫生报告撰写与政策倡导
本步骤的核心任务是撰写全球卫生研究报告，提出全球行动建议。需要综合研究发现，向国际组织、各国政府、民间社会等不同利益相关方传达研究结论，推动全球卫生治理和政策改进。全球卫生研究不仅是学术活动，更是推动社会变革的工具。

• 撰写研究报告：按照全球卫生报告规范撰写，包括执行摘要、研究背景、方法、主要发现、政策建议，面向不同受众（决策者、研究者、公众）制作不同版本的报告
• 提出行动框架：基于研究结果提出全球/区域/国家层面的行动建议，制定路线图和时间表，明确责任主体和资源需求，提出监测与评估框架
• 政策倡导与传播：制定知识转化和传播策略，制作政策简报、信息图表、视频等传播材料，利用国际会议、媒体、社交媒体等渠道传播研究发现，推动政策对话和变革
产出：全球卫生研究报告、政策简报、行动框架建议、传播材料｜ 质量标准：报告科学严谨，行动建议具体可行，传播策略有效，政策影响力强

Steps

Step 1
Global Health Issue Selection and Study Design
The core task of this step is to select an important global health issue and design a multinational/cross-regional epidemiological study plan. Choose from global burden of disease, health equity, infectious disease control, NCDs.

• Select global health topic: choose from Global Burden of Disease (GBD) research, MDG/SDG-related health issues, emerging/re-emerging infectious diseases, cross-national health equity, global health security
• Study design: determine study type (multinational cross-sectional, international cohort, multi-center clinical trial, ecological study); select study countries/regions (different income levels, regions, health systems); define comparative framework
• Ethics and collaboration: consider international research ethics (WHO ethics guidelines, IRB requirements in different countries); establish international collaboration mechanisms; clarify data sharing agreements; respect local culture and community participation
Deliverable: Global health research design proposal (background, research questions, study design, country selection, ethical framework, collaboration mechanisms) | Quality standard: Globally relevant research topic, design considering cross-national differences, comprehensive ethical framework, feasible collaboration mechanisms
Step 2
Global Data Collection and Standardization
The core task of this step is to systematically collect global/multinational data and ensure data standardization and comparability. Global health research data comes from diverse sources.

• Data source identification: determine data sources—WHO/UNICEF/World Bank international databases, GBD database, national health statistics yearbooks, multinational surveys (DHS, STEPwise), published literature systematic reviews
• Data standardization: unify disease definitions and diagnostic criteria (ICD codes, WHO standards); unify measurement units and methods; perform PPP currency conversion; age/sex structure standardization (direct/indirect methods)
• Data quality assessment: assess national data quality (completeness, reliability, timeliness); identify missing and anomalous data; establish data quality rating system; document data limitations and uncertainty
Deliverable: Global health dataset (multinational standardized data, data dictionary, quality assessment report, standardization methodology) | Quality standard: Authoritative and reliable data sources, scientific standardization methods, comprehensive data quality assessment, strong comparability
Step 3
Global Burden of Disease and Health Equity Analysis
The core task of this step is to analyze global/regional burden of disease and assess health equity status. Calculate disease burden indicators (DALY, YLL, YLD), analyze social determinants of health.

• Disease burden analysis: calculate global/regional/national incidence, prevalence, mortality, DALY, YLL, YLD; analyze disease spectrum changes and epidemiological transition
• Health equity analysis: decompose health indicator differences by socioeconomic dimensions (income level, gender, education, residence); calculate health inequality indices (Gini coefficient, concentration index, slope index); analyze impact of social determinants on health
• Trends and forecasting: analyze global health trends over past 20-30 years; use time series models and population projection models to forecast future disease burden; assess progress toward SDG targets
Deliverable: Global burden of disease and health equity analysis report (burden estimates, inequality indices, trend forecasting, regional comparison) | Quality standard: Standard burden of disease estimation methods, comprehensive equity analysis dimensions, scientific trend forecasting
Step 4
Global Health Intervention Effectiveness Evaluation and Policy Analysis
The core task of this step is to evaluate effectiveness of global health interventions and analyze health policy impacts. Systematically assess implementation effectiveness and cost-effectiveness of interventions across countries.

• Intervention effectiveness evaluation: select key interventions (vaccination, antiviral therapy, tobacco control, salt iodization, maternal-child health programs); evaluate effectiveness using meta-analysis, systematic review, quasi-experimental designs; calculate NNT, vaccine effectiveness (VE)
• Cost-effectiveness analysis: perform CEA, CUA, CBA; calculate ICER; assess affordability and sustainability of interventions
• Policy analysis: analyze implementation effects of health policies in different countries; identify success factors and lessons learned; compare performance of different health system models (WHO health system framework: service delivery, workforce, information, financing, leadership); propose policy recommendations
Deliverable: Global health intervention evaluation and policy analysis report (intervention effectiveness evidence, cost-effectiveness analysis, policy comparison, recommendations) | Quality standard: Sufficient intervention evaluation evidence, standard cost-effectiveness analysis, in-depth and insightful policy analysis
Step 5
Global Health Report Writing and Policy Advocacy
The core task of this step is to write a global health research report and propose global action recommendations. Synthesize research findings and communicate conclusions to various stakeholders.

• Write research report: follow global health report standards including executive summary, background, methods, main findings, policy recommendations; produce different report versions for different audiences (policymakers, researchers, public)
• Propose action framework: based on research results, propose global/regional/national action recommendations; develop roadmap and timeline; clarify responsible actors and resource needs; propose monitoring and evaluation framework
• Policy advocacy and communication: develop knowledge translation and communication strategy; produce policy briefs, infographics, videos and other communication materials; use international conferences, media, social media to disseminate findings; promote policy dialogue and change
Deliverable: Global health research report, policy brief, action framework recommendations, communication materials | Quality standard: Scientific and rigorous report, specific and feasible action recommendations, effective communication strategy, strong policy influence
