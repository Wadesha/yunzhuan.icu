环境卫生

Environmental Health

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：流行病学、生物统计学 | 学期：第三学期

本课程系统介绍环境因素对人群健康的影响及预防控制策略。内容涵盖大气污染与健康、水污染与健康、土壤污染与健康、噪声与辐射、食品安全、室内空气质量、环境流行病学方法、环境毒理学基础、环境风险评估以及环境健康政策法规。通过案例讨论和实践项目，培养学生识别、评估和控制环境健康危害的能力。

This course systematically introduces the impact of environmental factors on population health and prevention control strategies. Topics include air pollution and health, water pollution and health, soil pollution and health, noise and radiation, food safety, indoor air quality, environmental epidemiology methods, environmental toxicology basics, environmental risk assessment, and environmental health policies and regulations. Through case discussions and practical projects, students develop skills in identifying, assessing, and controlling environmental health hazards.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：社区环境健康风险评估与干预方案

选择一个社区或环境问题（如大气污染、水污染或食品安全），进行环境暴露评估和健康风险分析，提出干预方案。撰写评估报告。

Select a community or environmental issue (such as air pollution, water pollution, or food safety), conduct environmental exposure assessment and health risk analysis, and propose intervention plans. Write an assessment report.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

示例：调查一个环境污染对健康的影响，比如某工业区周边居民的PM2.5暴露与呼吸系统疾病。你需要设计横断面调查问卷，收集居民健康状况和居住信息，用便携式空气质量监测仪获取暴露数据，用Logistic回归分析暴露-反应关系，最后计算归因风险并提出环境干预建议。
步骤 1
环境污染物选择与暴露评估方案设计
本步骤的核心任务是选择研究的环境污染物，设计科学的暴露评估方案。需要从众多环境污染物中选择具有重要公共卫生意义的污染物，确定暴露途径和暴露人群，选择合适的暴露评估方法。准确的暴露评估是环境流行病学研究的关键环节。

• 选择研究污染物：从大气污染物（PM2.5、PM10、SO2、NO2、O3、VOCs）、水污染物（重金属、有机物、微生物）、土壤污染物、噪声等中选择一种或一类污染物
• 确定暴露途径与人群：分析污染物的环境介质（空气、水、土壤、食物）和暴露途径（呼吸道、消化道、皮肤接触），确定目标人群（一般人群、职业人群、敏感人群如儿童/孕妇）
• 设计暴露评估方案：选择暴露评估方法（环境监测、个体采样、生物监测、问卷调查、空间分析/地理信息系统GIS），确定监测点、监测频率、监测指标和样本量
产出：环境流行病学研究设计方案（含污染物选择依据、暴露途径分析、暴露评估方法、监测方案）｜ 质量标准：污染物选择有公共卫生意义，暴露评估方法科学合理，监测方案详细可行
步骤 2
环境暴露数据采集与处理
本步骤的核心任务是系统采集环境污染物数据和人群暴露数据，进行数据处理和质量控制。需要使用专业的环境监测设备和生物监测方法，确保数据的准确性和可靠性。环境暴露数据的质量直接影响研究结果的有效性。

• 环境监测：使用大气采样器（中流量/大流量）、个体采样泵采集空气污染物，使用水质自动监测仪或实验室分析（ICP-MS、GC-MS）检测水污染物，记录监测时间、地点、气象条件
• 生物监测：采集人体生物样本（血液、尿液、头发、母乳），检测内暴露剂量（血铅、尿镉、呼出气NO、血清代谢物），使用液相色谱-质谱联用（LC-MS/MS）等技术
• 数据处理与质控：对监测数据进行校准、背景校正、检出限处理，计算时间加权平均浓度（TWA），评估数据完整性（有效率≥90%），进行实验室内部质量控制和外部质量评价
产出：环境暴露数据集（含环境监测数据、生物监测数据、数据处理报告、质量控制报告）｜ 质量标准：监测方法规范，数据准确可靠，质量控制完善，数据完整性高
步骤 3
健康效应数据收集与健康结局评估
本步骤的核心任务是收集人群健康效应数据，评估环境污染物暴露对健康的影响。需要选择敏感的健康效应终点指标，采用客观的评估方法，建立暴露-反应关系。健康结局的准确评估是判断环境污染物健康危害的核心依据。

• 健康结局选择：选择与污染物相关的健康结局——急性效应（呼吸系统症状、肺功能下降、心血管事件）、慢性效应（慢性疾病发病率/死亡率、癌症、出生缺陷）、亚临床效应（生物标志物、炎症因子、氧化应激指标）
• 健康数据收集：通过医疗机构病历、疾病监测系统、健康体检数据、问卷调查（症状/疾病史）、体格检查和实验室检查（肺功能测定、血压测量、血液生化）收集健康数据
• 健康结局评估：使用标准化诊断标准确定病例，计算发病率、患病率、死亡率等频率指标，评估健康效应的时间模式和人群分布特征
产出：健康效应数据集（含健康结局资料、生物标志物数据、健康评估报告）｜ 质量标准：健康结局定义明确，数据来源可靠，评估方法标准，诊断依据充分
步骤 4
暴露-反应关系分析与健康风险评估
本步骤的核心任务是分析环境暴露与健康效应之间的暴露-反应关系，进行定量健康风险评估。暴露-反应关系是环境流行病学研究的核心内容，是制定环境质量标准和风险管理措施的科学依据。健康风险评估为决策提供量化依据。

• 暴露-反应关系分析：使用回归模型（线性回归、logistic回归、Cox回归）分析暴露剂量与健康效应的关系，估计单位暴露增量对应的健康风险（如PM2.5每升高10μg/m³的心血管疾病风险增加百分比）
• 健康风险评估：按照风险评估四步法（危害鉴定、剂量-反应关系评估、暴露评估、风险表征）进行定量健康风险评估，计算归因风险（AR）和人群归因风险百分比（PAR%），使用生命表法计算伤残调整寿命年（DALY）
• 模型构建与预测：构建时间序列模型（广义相加模型GAM）、空间回归模型、CGE模型等，预测不同污染水平下的健康影响，绘制风险地图，识别高风险区域和高风险人群
产出：暴露-反应关系与健康风险评估报告（含暴露-反应曲线、风险定量估计、风险地图、不确定性分析）｜ 质量标准：暴露-反应关系分析方法正确，风险评估规范，定量结果可靠，不确定性分析充分
步骤 5
环境流行病学研究报告与政策建议
本步骤的核心任务是撰写环境流行病学研究报告，提出基于证据的政策建议。需要系统总结研究发现，评估环境污染物的健康危害，提出针对性的污染防控和人群健康保护措施。研究报告应为环境政策制定和公共卫生实践提供科学支撑。

• 撰写研究报告：按照环境流行病学研究规范撰写完整报告，包括研究背景、目的、方法、结果、讨论、结论、建议，系统总结污染物暴露水平、健康效应、暴露-反应关系
• 政策建议制定：基于研究结果提出环境管理政策建议（环境质量标准修订、排放控制措施、健康防护指南），针对不同人群（一般人群、敏感人群）提出差异化保护措施，评估干预措施的成本效益
• 科普与健康传播：制作科普材料和健康指南，面向公众宣传环境健康知识，提出个人防护建议（如空气质量指数AQI发布与防护指引），提高公众环境健康素养
产出：环境流行病学研究报告、政策建议报告、健康科普材料｜ 质量标准：研究报告科学严谨，政策建议针对性强、可操作，科普材料通俗易懂

Steps

Step 1
Environmental Pollutant Selection and Exposure Assessment Design
The core task of this step is to select environmental pollutants for study and design a scientific exposure assessment plan. Choose pollutants with significant public health importance from numerous environmental pollutants.

• Select study pollutants: choose from air pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, SO2, NO2, O3, VOCs), water pollutants (heavy metals, organic compounds, microbes), soil pollutants, noise, etc.
• Determine exposure routes and populations: analyze environmental media (air, water, soil, food) and exposure pathways (respiratory, digestive, skin contact); define target population (general, occupational, sensitive groups like children/pregnant women)
• Design exposure assessment plan: select exposure assessment methods (environmental monitoring, personal sampling, biomonitoring, questionnaire survey, spatial analysis/GIS); determine monitoring sites, frequency, indicators, and sample size
Deliverable: Environmental epidemiology research design proposal (pollutant selection rationale, exposure pathway analysis, exposure assessment methods, monitoring plan) | Quality standard: Public health-relevant pollutant selection, scientific and reasonable exposure assessment methods, detailed and feasible monitoring plan
Step 2
Environmental Exposure Data Collection and Processing
The core task of this step is to systematically collect environmental pollutant data and human exposure data, perform data processing and quality control. Use professional environmental monitoring equipment and biomonitoring methods.

• Environmental monitoring: use air samplers (medium/high flow), personal sampling pumps for air pollutants; use water quality auto-monitors or lab analysis (ICP-MS, GC-MS) for water pollutants; record monitoring time, location, meteorological conditions
• Biomonitoring: collect human biological samples (blood, urine, hair, breast milk); detect internal exposure dose (blood lead, urine cadmium, exhaled NO, serum metabolites); use techniques like liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS)
• Data processing and QC: calibrate monitoring data, background correction, detection limit processing; calculate time-weighted average (TWA); assess data completeness (valid rate ≥90%); perform internal lab QC and external quality assessment
Deliverable: Environmental exposure dataset (environmental monitoring data, biomonitoring data, data processing report, quality control report) | Quality standard: Standard monitoring methods, accurate and reliable data, comprehensive quality control, high data completeness
Step 3
Health Effect Data Collection and Health Outcome Assessment
The core task of this step is to collect population health effect data and assess health impacts of environmental pollutant exposure. Select sensitive health effect endpoint indicators and use objective assessment methods.

• Health outcome selection: choose pollutant-relevant health outcomes—acute effects (respiratory symptoms, lung function decline, cardiovascular events), chronic effects (chronic disease incidence/mortality, cancer, birth defects), subclinical effects (biomarkers, inflammatory factors, oxidative stress indicators)
• Health data collection: collect health data through medical records, disease surveillance systems, health checkup data, questionnaire surveys (symptoms/disease history), physical examinations and lab tests (pulmonary function test, blood pressure measurement, blood biochemistry)
• Health outcome assessment: use standardized diagnostic criteria to define cases; calculate frequency indicators (incidence, prevalence, mortality); assess temporal patterns and population distribution characteristics of health effects
Deliverable: Health effect dataset (health outcome data, biomarker data, health assessment report) | Quality standard: Clear health outcome definition, reliable data sources, standard assessment methods, sufficient diagnostic evidence
Step 4
Exposure-Response Relationship Analysis and Health Risk Assessment
The core task of this step is to analyze the exposure-response relationship between environmental exposure and health effects, and perform quantitative health risk assessment. Exposure-response relationship is the core content of environmental epidemiology.

• Exposure-response relationship analysis: use regression models (linear, logistic, Cox) to analyze relationship between exposure dose and health effects; estimate health risk per unit exposure increment (e.g., percent increase in cardiovascular disease risk per 10μg/m³ PM2.5 increase)
• Health risk assessment: perform quantitative health risk assessment following four-step risk assessment (hazard identification, dose-response assessment, exposure assessment, risk characterization); calculate attributable risk (AR) and population attributable risk percent (PAR%); use life table method to calculate DALY
• Model building and prediction: build time series models (generalized additive model GAM), spatial regression models, CGE models; predict health impacts under different pollution levels; draw risk maps; identify high-risk areas and populations
Deliverable: Exposure-response relationship and health risk assessment report (exposure-response curves, quantitative risk estimates, risk maps, uncertainty analysis) | Quality standard: Correct exposure-response analysis methods, standard risk assessment, reliable quantitative results, comprehensive uncertainty analysis
Step 5
Environmental Epidemiology Research Report and Policy Recommendations
The core task of this step is to write an environmental epidemiology research report and propose evidence-based policy recommendations. Systematically summarize research findings, assess health hazards of environmental pollutants.

• Write research report: compose complete report following environmental epidemiology research standards, including background, objectives, methods, results, discussion, conclusions, recommendations; systematically summarize exposure levels, health effects, exposure-response relationships
• Policy recommendation development: propose environmental management policy recommendations based on research results (environmental quality standard revision, emission control measures, health protection guidelines); propose differentiated protection measures for different populations (general, sensitive); assess cost-effectiveness of interventions
• Science communication and health promotion: create popular science materials and health guidelines; educate public on environmental health knowledge; propose personal protection recommendations (e.g., AQI release and protection guidelines); improve public environmental health literacy
Deliverable: Environmental epidemiology research report, policy recommendation report, health popular science materials | Quality standard: Scientific and rigorous research report, targeted and actionable policy recommendations, accessible popular science materials
