流行病学

Epidemiology

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：生物统计学基础 | 学期：第二学期

本课程是公共卫生专业的核心基础课，系统介绍流行病学的基本概念、原理和方法。内容涵盖疾病分布描述、常用流行病学研究方法（队列研究、病例对照研究、横断面研究、实验流行病学）、偏倚与混杂、因果推断、疾病监测以及突发公共卫生事件调查。通过案例分析和实践练习，培养学生运用流行病学思维解决公共卫生问题的能力。

This core course introduces fundamental concepts, principles, and methods of epidemiology. Topics include disease distribution, common study designs (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional, experimental), bias and confounding, causal inference, disease surveillance, and outbreak investigation. Through case studies and practical exercises, students develop epidemiological thinking and problem-solving skills in public health.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：某地区传染病流行病学调查与数据分析

选择一种传染病，设计流行病学调查方案，收集并分析疾病分布数据，探讨危险因素和传播途径。撰写完整流行病学调查报告。

Select an infectious disease, design an epidemiological investigation plan, collect and analyze disease distribution data, and explore risk factors and transmission routes. Write a complete epidemiological investigation report.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

示例：调查一个具体的疾病，比如校园里的流感传播。你需要收集病例数据（发病时间、地点、接触史），绘制流行曲线和传播链，计算基本再生数R0，然后提出防控建议。
步骤 1
传染病选择与流行病学调查设计
本步骤的核心任务是选择一种重要的传染病，设计科学的流行病学调查方案。需要综合考虑疾病的公共卫生重要性、数据可获得性和调查可行性，选择合适的流行病学研究方法（描述性研究、分析性研究或实验流行病学）。调查设计的科学性是获得可靠结论的基础。

• 从病毒性肝炎、结核病、艾滋病、流感、登革热、手足口病等中选择一种传染病，了解其病原体特征、流行概况和研究进展
• 设计调查方案：确定研究类型（现况调查、病例对照研究、队列研究），明确研究对象、样本量计算、抽样方法、调查内容和观察指标，制定调查时间表
• 设计调查工具：设计调查问卷（人口学信息、暴露史、临床信息、实验室检查），制定数据收集表格和质量控制方案，列出所需器材和伦理审查要点
产出：流行病学调查设计方案（含研究背景、研究目的、研究设计、样本量计算、调查工具、质量控制、伦理考虑）｜ 质量标准：研究设计科学合理，方法选择恰当，样本量计算正确，质量控制措施完善
步骤 2
数据收集与三间分布描述
本步骤的核心任务是系统收集疾病分布数据，描述疾病在人群、时间和空间上的分布特征。三间分布描述是流行病学研究的基础，能够提供病因线索、确定高危人群、评价防治效果。数据收集需要注意完整性和准确性。

• 数据收集：通过公共卫生监测系统、医院病历、现场调查等方式收集病例资料，包括人口学特征、发病时间、发病地点、临床表现、实验室检测结果等
• 人群分布分析：按年龄、性别、职业、种族、婚姻状况等人群特征分组，计算不同组别的发病率、患病率、死亡率，绘制人群分布直方图或饼图
• 时间分布分析：按年、季、月、周、日分析发病变化趋势，绘制流行曲线（epidemic curve），判断流行类型（暴发、季节性、周期性、长期趋势）
• 地区分布分析：按不同地理区域（城乡、不同省市、不同社区）统计发病情况，绘制标点地图或热力图，分析聚集性和传播范围
产出：三间分布描述报告（含人群/时间/地区分布数据表、统计图、流行曲线、地图可视化）｜ 质量标准：数据完整准确，分布描述全面，图表规范清晰，分析深入
步骤 3
危险因素分析与病因推断
本步骤的核心任务是分析疾病的危险因素，进行病因推断。需要运用流行病学分析方法（病例对照研究、队列研究）筛选危险因素，评估暴露与疾病的关联强度，并应用病因推断标准进行因果推断。危险因素分析是制定防控策略的科学依据。

• 筛选危险因素：从宿主因素（年龄、性别、遗传、免疫状态）、环境因素（居住条件、卫生设施、空气污染）、行为因素（饮食习惯、吸烟饮酒、个人卫生）、社会因素（经济水平、教育程度、医疗条件）等方面筛选可能的危险因素
• 关联强度分析：计算相对危险度（RR）、比值比（OR）、归因危险度（AR）、人群归因危险度百分比（PAR%），进行卡方检验和趋势性检验，确定统计学关联
• 病因推断：应用Hill病因推断标准（关联强度、一致性、时间顺序、剂量反应关系、实验证据、生物学合理性等）进行综合判断，绘制病因链和病因网模型
产出：危险因素分析报告（含单因素分析表、多因素分析结果、关联强度指标、病因推断）｜ 质量标准：统计方法正确，关联强度指标计算准确，病因推断逻辑严谨
步骤 4
传播机制分析与防控策略制定
本步骤的核心任务是分析疾病的传播机制和流行规律，制定针对性的防控策略和措施。需要基于传染病的传染源、传播途径和易感人群三个基本环节，结合调查结果分析传播链和传播模式，提出综合性防控措施。防控策略应具有科学性、可行性和成本效益。

• 传播机制分析：确定传染源（患者、隐性感染者、病原携带者、动物宿主）、传播途径（呼吸道、消化道、接触、虫媒、血液母婴等）、易感人群特征，分析传播动力学基本再生数R0
• 流行因素分析：分析自然因素（气候、地理、媒介生物）和社会因素（人口流动、卫生条件、医疗资源、防控政策）对流行的影响，预测流行趋势
• 制定防控策略：针对三个环节提出防控措施（管理传染源、切断传播途径、保护易感人群），制定监测方案、应急响应预案、健康教育计划，评估成本效益
产出：防控策略方案（含传播机制分析、流行因素分析、防控措施清单、应急预案、监测方案）｜ 质量标准：传播机制分析准确，防控措施针对性强，方案科学可行
步骤 5
流行病学调查报告撰写
本步骤的核心任务是撰写完整的流行病学调查报告，系统呈现调查设计、调查方法、主要发现和防控建议。流行病学调查报告是公共卫生决策的重要依据，需要结构清晰、数据准确、结论可靠、建议可行。报告撰写体现流行病学思维和专业能力。

• 按照流行病学调查报告规范撰写：摘要、背景、调查目的、调查方法、调查结果、分析与讨论、防控建议、参考文献，字数不少于3500字
• 制作高质量图表：三间分布图表、流行曲线、地区分布图、危险因素分析表、森林图、病因链示意图
• 深入讨论：总结主要发现，讨论调查的局限性（偏倚、混杂），比较与其他研究的异同，提出进一步研究方向和政策建议
产出：完整流行病学调查报告（PDF格式，含全部调查结果和分析）、数据表格附件、图表集｜ 质量标准：报告结构完整、数据可靠、分析深入、结论有据、建议可行、格式规范

Steps

Step 1
Infectious Disease Selection and Epidemiological Investigation Design
The core task of this step is to select an important infectious disease and design a scientific epidemiological investigation plan. It requires comprehensive consideration of public health importance, data availability, and feasibility.

• Choose an infectious disease from viral hepatitis, tuberculosis, AIDS, influenza, dengue fever, hand-foot-mouth disease, etc.; learn about pathogen characteristics, epidemic overview, and research progress
• Design investigation plan: determine study type (cross-sectional, case-control, cohort study); define study subjects, sample size calculation, sampling method, survey content, and observation indicators; develop investigation timeline
• Design survey tools: design questionnaire (demographic information, exposure history, clinical information, laboratory tests); develop data collection forms and quality control plan; list required equipment and ethical review considerations
Deliverable: Epidemiological investigation design proposal (background, objectives, study design, sample size calculation, survey tools, quality control, ethical considerations) | Quality standard: Scientific and reasonable study design, appropriate method selection, correct sample size calculation, comprehensive quality control measures
Step 2
Data Collection and Three-Dimensional Distribution Description
The core task of this step is to systematically collect disease distribution data and describe disease characteristics by person, time, and place. Three-dimensional distribution description is the foundation of epidemiological research.

• Data collection: collect case data through public health surveillance systems, hospital records, field investigations; include demographic characteristics, onset time, location, clinical manifestations, laboratory test results
• Person distribution analysis: group by age, gender, occupation, ethnicity, marital status; calculate incidence, prevalence, mortality by group; draw population distribution histograms or pie charts
• Time distribution analysis: analyze incidence trends by year, quarter, month, week, day; draw epidemic curves; determine epidemic types (outbreak, seasonal, periodic, secular trend)
• Place distribution analysis: statistics incidence by geographic areas (urban/rural, different provinces/cities, communities); draw dot maps or heatmaps; analyze clustering and transmission range
Deliverable: Three-dimensional distribution description report (person/time/place distribution data tables, statistical charts, epidemic curves, geographic visualization) | Quality standard: Complete and accurate data, comprehensive distribution description, standard and clear charts, in-depth analysis
Step 3
Risk Factor Analysis and Causal Inference
The core task of this step is to analyze disease risk factors and perform causal inference. Use epidemiological analysis methods (case-control, cohort studies) to screen risk factors, assess association strength between exposure and disease.

• Screen risk factors: identify potential risk factors from host factors (age, gender, genetics, immune status), environmental factors (housing conditions, sanitation facilities, air pollution), behavioral factors (diet, smoking/drinking, personal hygiene), social factors (economic level, education, medical resources)
• Association strength analysis: calculate relative risk (RR), odds ratio (OR), attributable risk (AR), population attributable risk percent (PAR%); perform chi-square tests and trend tests; determine statistical association
• Causal inference: apply Hill's criteria for causation (strength of association, consistency, temporality, dose-response relationship, experimental evidence, biological plausibility, etc.) for comprehensive judgment; draw causal chain and web of causation models
Deliverable: Risk factor analysis report (univariate analysis table, multivariate analysis results, association strength indicators, causal inference) | Quality standard: Correct statistical methods, accurate association strength calculation, rigorous causal inference logic
Step 4
Transmission Mechanism Analysis and Prevention Strategy Development
The core task of this step is to analyze disease transmission mechanisms and epidemic patterns, and develop targeted prevention and control strategies and measures. Based on the three basic links of infectious diseases (source, route, susceptible population).

• Transmission mechanism analysis: identify sources of infection (patients, asymptomatic carriers, pathogen carriers, animal reservoirs), transmission routes (respiratory, digestive, contact, vector-borne, blood/maternal-fetal), susceptible population characteristics; analyze basic reproduction number R0 of transmission dynamics
• Epidemic factor analysis: analyze impacts of natural factors (climate, geography, vectors) and social factors (population mobility, sanitation conditions, medical resources, prevention policies); predict epidemic trends
• Develop prevention strategies: propose control measures targeting three links (managing sources, cutting transmission routes, protecting susceptible populations); develop surveillance plans, emergency response plans, health education programs; assess cost-effectiveness
Deliverable: Prevention and control strategy plan (transmission mechanism analysis, epidemic factor analysis, prevention measures list, emergency plan, surveillance plan) | Quality standard: Accurate transmission mechanism analysis, targeted prevention measures, scientific and feasible plan
Step 5
Epidemiological Investigation Report Writing
The core task of this step is to write a complete epidemiological investigation report systematically presenting investigation design, methods, main findings, and prevention recommendations. Epidemiological investigation reports are important basis for public health decision-making.

• Write following epidemiological investigation report standards: abstract, background, objectives, methods, results, analysis and discussion, prevention recommendations, references; minimum 3500 words
• Create high-quality figures and tables: three-dimensional distribution charts, epidemic curves, geographic distribution maps, risk factor analysis tables, forest plots, causal chain diagrams
• In-depth discussion: summarize main findings; discuss study limitations (bias, confounding); compare with other studies; propose further research directions and policy recommendations
Deliverable: Complete epidemiological investigation report (PDF format with all results and analysis), data table appendices, figure collection | Quality standard: Complete structure, reliable data, in-depth analysis, evidence-based conclusions, feasible recommendations, professional format
