特殊教育教学法

Special Education Teaching Methods

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：特殊教育导论 | 学期：春季

本课程聚焦特殊教育的教学策略与方法，培养学生设计和实施有效教学的能力。课程内容包括：个别化教学的设计与实施、任务分析与链式教学、直接教学法、合作学习策略、辅助技术的应用、课堂管理与环境创设、以及差异化教学。学生将学习如何根据学生的个别差异调整课程内容、教学方法和评价方式，掌握各学科领域（语文、数学、社会适应等）的特殊教学策略。

This course focuses on instructional strategies and methods in special education, developing students' ability to design and implement effective instruction. Topics include individualized instructional design, task analysis and chaining, direct instruction, cooperative learning strategies, assistive technology applications, classroom management and environmental design, and differentiated instruction. Students learn to adapt curriculum, methods, and assessment to individual differences, and acquire subject-specific strategies for language arts, mathematics, and social adaptation.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：个别化教学设计与实施

针对一名特殊需要学生，运用任务分析、直接教学法等方法设计一份个别化教学方案，并进行模拟教学实施。

For a student with special needs, use task analysis and direct instruction methods to design an individualized teaching plan, and conduct simulated teaching implementation.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

📋 示例：为一名有注意力缺陷多动障碍（ADHD）的小学生设计一节40分钟的数学课。你需要运用任务分析法将教学目标分解为小步骤，设计多感官教学活动（视觉提示、动手操作、肢体运动），安排结构化的课堂常规和注意力信号系统，准备自我监控清单和代币奖励系统，课后反思教学调整的有效性。
步骤 1
教学对象分析与教学目标设定
本步骤的核心任务是深入分析特殊儿童的学习特点和个体差异，设定个别化的教学目标。特殊儿童之间的个体差异远大于普通儿童，"一刀切"的教学方式对他们往往是无效的。教学目标的设定必须基于评估，充分考虑儿童的现有水平和最近发展区。

• 运用生态评估和功能性评估方法，全面分析特殊儿童的认知特点、学习风格、优势领域、困难领域、感知觉特点、注意力特点、情绪行为特点等
• 基于评估结果，按照最近发展区理论设定个别化教学目标，区分长期目标、短期目标和具体教学目标，确保目标具有挑战性又可达成
• 对教学目标进行任务分析，将复杂的目标分解为一系列小的、连续的子技能，确定教学的先后顺序和递进关系，为教学设计提供清晰路径
产出：学习者分析与教学目标（含学习者特征分析、目标体系、任务分析）｜ 质量标准：分析深入细致，目标具体可测，任务分解科学
步骤 2
教学策略选择与教学设计
本步骤的核心任务是选择适合特殊儿童的教学策略，进行系统的教学设计。特殊教育有其专门的教学方法和策略，如应用行为分析、直接教学、精准教学、支架式教学、同伴介导教学等。方法的选择应基于儿童的障碍类型、学习特点和教学目标。

• 根据教学目标和儿童特点，选择合适的教学方法和策略，如应用行为分析（ABA）、直接教学、精准教学、支架式教学、认知策略教学、自然情境教学等
• 设计详细的教学活动方案，包括教学内容呈现方式、教学步骤、教学材料、辅助方式、强化策略、纠错程序等，确保教学的系统性和规范性
• 设计差异化和个别化的教学调整方案，包括内容调整、难度调整、呈现方式调整、时间调整、评价方式调整等，满足不同儿童的特殊需要
产出：教学设计方案集（含教学策略说明、详细教案、差异化调整方案）｜ 质量标准：策略选择适切，设计系统详细，个别化程度高
步骤 3
教学材料与辅助技术开发
本步骤的核心任务是开发和准备适合特殊儿童的教学材料与辅助技术。合适的教学材料和辅助技术能够弥补特殊儿童的功能缺陷，支持他们的学习和参与。通用设计学习（UDL）理念强调从设计之初就考虑多样性的需求，为所有学习者提供支持。

• 依据通用设计学习（UDL）的三大原则（多元表征、多元行动与表达、多元投入）设计和改编教学材料，确保材料的可及性和适应性
• 为不同障碍类型的儿童开发适配的教学材料，如视力障碍儿童的盲文/大字材料、听力障碍儿童的手语/字幕材料、智力障碍儿童的具象化/结构化材料等
• 选择和应用合适的辅助技术，包括低科技辅助（视觉提示、图形日程、沟通板）和高科技辅助（语音合成、特殊软件、AAC辅助沟通系统）
产出：教学材料与辅助技术方案（含材料包、辅助技术清单、使用指南）｜ 质量标准：材料丰富适切，辅助技术有效，符合UDL理念
步骤 4
教学实施与动态调整
本步骤的核心任务是实施个别化教学，并根据儿童的反应动态调整教学策略。教学实施是检验教学设计有效性的过程，特殊儿童的学习进展往往是不均衡的，需要教师具有高度的敏感性和灵活性，及时调整教学节奏和方法。

• 按照教学设计实施方案教学，在教学过程中密切观察儿童的反应，使用数据收集系统记录儿童的学习表现和进步情况
• 运用形成性评价和进展监测（progress monitoring）技术，每周定期评估儿童的学习进展，用数据驱动教学决策
• 根据儿童的学习进展动态调整教学策略，如果连续3-5次数据显示没有进步，就要及时分析原因，调整教学内容、方法或难度，确保教学的有效性
产出：教学实施与动态调整记录（含教学日志、进展数据、调整记录）｜ 质量标准：实施规范，数据驱动，调整及时有效
步骤 5
教学效果评价与教学反思
本步骤的核心任务是全面评价教学效果，进行深度的教学反思和专业总结。特殊教育的教学评价不仅要看目标达成度，还要看儿童的学习效率、参与度、泛化程度和生活质量的提升。反思是特殊教育教师专业成长的核心途径。

• 运用多元评价方法（标准化测验、课程本位评估、档案袋评价、真实性评价、社会效度评价）全面评价教学效果，考察目标达成度、技能泛化、维持效果和社会意义
• 从教学目标、教学内容、教学方法、教学材料、教学节奏、师生互动等多个维度进行教学反思，总结成功经验和不足之处
• 基于反思结果提出改进方案，形成"设计-实施-评价-反思-改进"的教学研究循环，不断提升特殊教育教学质量
产出：教学评价与反思报告（含评价结果、反思记录、改进方案）｜ 质量标准：评价全面深入，反思深刻具体，改进方向明确

Steps

Step 1
Teaching Object Analysis and Teaching Goal Setting
The core task of this step is to deeply analyze the learning characteristics and individual differences of children with special needs and set individualized teaching goals. Individual differences among children with special needs are far greater than among typical children; "one-size-fits-all" teaching methods are often ineffective for them. Teaching goal setting must be based on assessment, fully considering children's current levels and zones of proximal development.

• Use ecological assessment and functional assessment methods to comprehensively analyze children with special needs in terms of cognitive characteristics, learning styles, strength areas, difficulty areas, sensory-perceptual characteristics, attention characteristics, emotional and behavioral characteristics, etc.
• Based on assessment results, set individualized teaching goals according to the Zone of Proximal Development theory, distinguishing long-term goals, short-term goals, and specific teaching goals, ensuring goals are challenging yet achievable
• Conduct task analysis on teaching goals, decomposing complex goals into a series of small, continuous sub-skills, determining teaching sequence and progressive relationships, providing a clear path for teaching design
Deliverable: Learner Analysis and Teaching Goals (including learner characteristics analysis, goal system, task analysis) | Quality standard: In-depth and detailed analysis, specific and measurable goals, scientific task decomposition
Step 2
Teaching Strategy Selection and Teaching Design
The core task of this step is to select teaching strategies suitable for children with special needs and conduct systematic teaching design. Special education has its specialized teaching methods and strategies, such as Applied Behavior Analysis, direct instruction, precision teaching, scaffolded instruction, peer-mediated instruction, etc. Method selection should be based on children's disability type, learning characteristics, and teaching goals.

• According to teaching goals and children's characteristics, select appropriate teaching methods and strategies, such as Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA), direct instruction, precision teaching, scaffolded instruction, cognitive strategy instruction, naturalistic teaching, etc.
• Design detailed teaching activity plans, including teaching content presentation methods, teaching steps, teaching materials, prompting methods, reinforcement strategies, error correction procedures, etc., ensuring systematic and standardized teaching
• Design differentiated and individualized teaching adjustment plans, including content adjustment, difficulty adjustment, presentation method adjustment, time adjustment, evaluation method adjustment, etc., to meet the special needs of different children
Deliverable: Teaching Design Plan Collection (including teaching strategy explanation, detailed lesson plans, differentiated adjustment plans) | Quality standard: Appropriate strategy selection, systematic and detailed design, high degree of individualization
Step 3
Teaching Materials and Assistive Technology Development
The core task of this step is to develop and prepare teaching materials and assistive technology suitable for children with special needs. Appropriate teaching materials and assistive technology can compensate for functional deficits of children with special needs and support their learning and participation. The Universal Design for Learning (UDL) philosophy emphasizes considering diverse needs from the beginning of design to provide support for all learners.

• Design and adapt teaching materials according to the three principles of Universal Design for Learning (multiple representation, multiple action and expression, multiple engagement), ensuring accessibility and adaptability of materials
• Develop adapted teaching materials for children with different disability types, such as braille/large print materials for children with visual impairments, sign language/subtitle materials for children with hearing impairments, concrete/structured materials for children with intellectual disabilities, etc.
• Select and apply appropriate assistive technology, including low-tech assistive devices (visual prompts, picture schedules, communication boards) and high-tech assistive devices (speech synthesis, special software, AAC systems)
Deliverable: Teaching Materials and Assistive Technology Plan (including material package, assistive technology list, user guide) | Quality standard: Rich and appropriate materials, effective assistive technology, consistent with UDL philosophy
Step 4
Teaching Implementation and Dynamic Adjustment
The core task of this step is to implement individualized teaching and dynamically adjust teaching strategies according to children's responses. Teaching implementation is the process of testing the effectiveness of teaching design. Learning progress of children with special needs is often uneven, requiring teachers to have high sensitivity and flexibility to timely adjust teaching rhythm and methods.

• Implement teaching according to the teaching design plan, closely observe children's responses during teaching, and use data collection systems to record children's learning performance and progress
• Apply formative assessment and progress monitoring techniques, regularly assess children's learning progress weekly, and use data to drive instructional decisions
• Dynamically adjust teaching strategies based on children's learning progress; if data for 3-5 consecutive sessions show no progress, timely analyze reasons and adjust teaching content, methods, or difficulty to ensure teaching effectiveness
Deliverable: Teaching Implementation and Dynamic Adjustment Records (including teaching logs, progress data, adjustment records) | Quality standard: Standardized implementation, data-driven, timely and effective adjustment
Step 5
Teaching Effect Evaluation and Teaching Reflection
The core task of this step is to comprehensively evaluate teaching effects and conduct in-depth teaching reflection and professional summary. Teaching evaluation in special education should not only look at goal achievement but also at children's learning efficiency, participation, generalization degree, and improvement in quality of life. Reflection is the core pathway for special education teachers' professional growth.

• Use multiple evaluation methods (standardized tests, curriculum-based assessment, portfolio assessment, authentic assessment, social validity assessment) to comprehensively evaluate teaching effects, examining goal achievement, skill generalization, maintenance effects, and social significance
• Conduct teaching reflection from multiple dimensions: teaching goals, teaching content, teaching methods, teaching materials, teaching rhythm, teacher-child interaction, etc., summarizing successful experiences and shortcomings
• Based on reflection results, propose improvement plans, forming a teaching research cycle of "design-implementation-evaluation-reflection-improvement" to continuously improve special education teaching quality
Deliverable: Teaching Evaluation and Reflection Report (including evaluation results, reflection records, improvement plan) | Quality standard: Comprehensive and in-depth evaluation, profound and specific reflection, clear improvement direction
