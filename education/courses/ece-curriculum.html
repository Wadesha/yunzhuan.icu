幼儿课程设计

Early Childhood Curriculum Design

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：儿童发展心理学 | 学期：第二学年春季

本课程讲授幼儿园课程设计的基本原理与方法，涵盖课程目标设定、内容选择、活动组织、实施与评价等完整环节。学生将学习蒙台梭利、瑞吉欧、高瞻等经典课程模式，掌握主题活动、区域活动、项目活动等多种课程组织形式。课程强调以儿童为中心的教育理念，注重培养学生设计符合幼儿年龄特点和发展需求的教育活动的能力，通过小组合作和模拟教学提升实践技能。

This course teaches fundamental principles and methods of kindergarten curriculum design, covering goal setting, content selection, activity organization, implementation, and evaluation. Students will study classic curriculum models including Montessori, Reggio Emilia, and HighScope, and master thematic activities, learning centers, and project approaches. The course emphasizes child-centered education, developing students' ability to design age-appropriate educational activities through group work and simulated teaching.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：幼儿园主题活动方案设计与实施

基于蒙台梭利、瑞吉欧或高瞻等课程理念，设计一份完整的幼儿园主题活动方案，包含课程目标、主题网络图、活动安排、环境创设与评价方式。

Based on Montessori, Reggio Emilia, or HighScope curriculum philosophy, design a complete kindergarten thematic activity plan including goals, theme web, activity schedule, environment setup, and evaluation methods.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

📋 示例：设计一个幼儿园主题活动——从目标到实施，完整规划一周的活动内容。
步骤 1
课程理念选择与理论论证
本步骤的核心任务是选择一种课程理念作为设计基础，并进行深入的理论论证。课程理念是课程设计的灵魂，决定了课程目标、内容、方法和评价的整体方向。蒙台梭利、瑞吉欧、高瞻等经典课程模式各有其哲学基础和实践特点，选择时需结合教育对象和教育目标进行理性判断。

• 深入研究蒙台梭利（敏感期理论、有准备的环境）、瑞吉欧（项目活动、儿童的一百种语言）、高瞻（计划-实施-回顾、关键经验）三种课程模式的理论基础、核心观点、教育目标和实践策略
• 运用比较分析法，从儿童观、教育观、师生关系、课程组织形式、评价方式五个维度对不同课程模式进行对比分析，论证所选模式的适切性
• 撰写课程理念阐述报告，说明选择理由、核心理念、教育原则以及在本主题活动中的具体体现方式，确保课程设计有坚实的理论支撑
产出：课程理念选择与论证报告（含模式对比分析表、理念阐述、适配性论证）｜ 质量标准：理论理解准确深入，比较分析有维度，选择论证有说服力
步骤 2
主题网络构建与课程目标体系设计
本步骤的核心任务是构建主题网络图，设计完整的课程目标体系。主题网络是主题课程的骨架，它将主题与各领域的学习经验有机联结起来。课程目标是课程设计的起点和归宿，应体现全面发展理念，涵盖五大领域。

• 运用"头脑风暴-分类梳理-网络建构"三步法构建主题网络图，从核心主题出发，发散出健康、语言、社会、科学、艺术五大领域的子主题和具体活动
• 依据《3-6岁儿童学习与发展指南》，设计单元总目标、领域目标和具体活动目标三级目标体系，每条目标都包含行为主体、行为动词、行为条件和表现程度四个要素
• 运用目标分类学理论，将目标分为认知、技能、情感三个维度，确保目标的全面性和层次性，为后续评价提供明确依据
产出：主题网络与课程目标体系（含主题网络图、三级目标体系表、目标分类矩阵）｜ 质量标准：主题网络系统完整，目标体系层次清晰，符合指南精神
步骤 3
系列活动方案精细化设计
本步骤的核心任务是编写系列活动的详细方案，将课程目标转化为具体可操作的教学活动。活动是课程实施的基本载体，好的活动设计应体现儿童的主体性，促进其主动学习和建构。每个活动都应包含目标、准备、过程、延伸、评价五个基本要素。

• 设计8-10个系列活动，涵盖集体教学、区域活动、户外探索、生活渗透、家园共育多种组织形式，形成由浅入深、层层递进的活动序列
• 每个活动方案按照"活动目标-活动准备-活动过程-活动延伸-活动评价"五要素编写，过程部分包含导入、展开、结束三个环节，明确师幼互动的具体方式
• 运用支架式教学理论，为每个活动设计多层次的支持策略，包括材料支架、问题支架、同伴支架和教师支架，满足不同发展水平幼儿的学习需求
产出：系列活动设计方案集（含8-10个活动详案、活动序列图、分层指导策略表）｜ 质量标准：活动设计要素完整，过程流畅有层次，支持策略具体有效
步骤 4
学习环境与区域创设规划
本步骤的核心任务是规划与主题相适配的学习环境和区域活动设置。环境是重要的教育资源，瑞吉欧教育理念将环境称为"第三位教师"。区域活动是幼儿自主学习、主动探究的重要场所，材料投放的适宜性直接影响学习效果。

• 按照动静分区、干湿分区、开放与封闭结合的原则，规划美工区、建构区、角色游戏区、科学探究区、阅读区、益智区等活动区域的空间布局和面积比例
• 根据主题进展分阶段投放材料，按照"引入期-探索期-深入期-展示期"设计材料投放序列，体现层次性、开放性和探究性
• 设计主题墙、问题墙、作品展示墙等互动墙面，规划幼儿参与环境创设的方式，让环境成为记录和展示幼儿学习过程的载体
产出：环境创设与区域规划方案（含空间布局图、区域材料清单、主题墙设计图、分阶段材料投放表）｜ 质量标准：空间布局科学合理，材料投放有层次，环境具有互动性和教育性
步骤 5
课程评价方案系统设计
本步骤的核心任务是设计过程性与结果性相结合的课程评价方案。评价不是课程的终点，而是促进课程不断完善和幼儿持续发展的重要手段。幼儿教育评价应遵循过程性、发展性、多元性原则，关注幼儿在学习过程中的表现和进步。

• 构建多元化评价体系，综合运用观察法、作品分析法、档案袋评价、谈话法、家长评价等多种方式，从多个角度收集评价信息
• 设计幼儿发展评价指标，对应课程目标，从健康、语言、社会、科学、艺术五个领域制定具体、可观察的评价标准和等级描述
• 设计课程本身的评价方案，包括目标达成度、活动适宜性、环境有效性、家长满意度四个维度，为课程改进提供依据
产出：课程评价方案（含幼儿发展评价指标、评价工具包、课程质量评价表、评价实施指南）｜ 质量标准：评价体系多元全面，工具具体可操作，体现发展性评价理念

Steps

Step 1
Curriculum Philosophy Selection and Theoretical Justification
The core task of this step is to select a curriculum philosophy as the design foundation and conduct in-depth theoretical justification. Curriculum philosophy is the soul of curriculum design, determining the overall direction of curriculum goals, content, methods, and evaluation. Classic curriculum models such as Montessori, Reggio Emilia, and HighScope each have their philosophical foundations and practical characteristics; selection requires rational judgment considering educational subjects and goals.

• Deeply study the theoretical foundations, core concepts, educational goals, and practical strategies of three curriculum models: Montessori (sensitive period theory, prepared environment), Reggio Emilia (project approach, hundred languages of children), and HighScope (plan-do-review, key experiences)
• Use comparative analysis to compare different curriculum models from five dimensions: view of the child, view of education, teacher-child relationship, curriculum organization form, and evaluation method, justifying the suitability of the selected model
• Write a curriculum philosophy elaboration report, explaining selection rationale, core concepts, educational principles, and specific manifestations in this thematic activity, ensuring the curriculum design has solid theoretical support
Deliverable: Curriculum Philosophy Selection and Justification Report (including model comparison table, philosophy elaboration, suitability argumentation) | Quality standard: Accurate and in-depth theoretical understanding, dimensional comparative analysis, persuasive selection argumentation
Step 2
Theme Web Construction and Curriculum Goal System Design
The core task of this step is to construct the theme web and design a complete curriculum goal system. The theme web is the skeleton of thematic curriculum, organically connecting the theme with learning experiences across domains. Curriculum goals are the starting point and destination of curriculum design, should embody the concept of holistic development, and cover five developmental domains.

• Use the three-step method of "brainstorming - categorization - web construction" to build the theme web, starting from the core theme and branching out into sub-themes and specific activities across health, language, social, science, and art domains
• Based on the "Guide to Learning and Development for Children Aged 3-6", design a three-level goal system: unit goals, domain goals, and specific activity goals, with each goal containing four elements: behavior subject, action verb, behavior condition, and performance level
• Apply taxonomy theory to categorize goals into cognitive, skill, and affective dimensions, ensuring comprehensiveness and hierarchy of goals, providing clear basis for subsequent evaluation
Deliverable: Theme Web and Curriculum Goal System (including theme web, three-level goal system table, goal classification matrix) | Quality standard: Systematic and complete theme web, clearly hierarchical goal system, consistent with Guide principles
Step 3
Refined Design of Series Activity Plans
The core task of this step is to write detailed plans for series activities, transforming curriculum goals into concrete and operable teaching activities. Activities are the basic carrier of curriculum implementation; good activity design should reflect children's subjectivity and promote their active learning and construction. Each activity should include five basic elements: objectives, preparation, process, extension, and evaluation.

• Design 8-10 series activities covering various organizational forms: collective teaching, learning centers, outdoor exploration, life integration, and home-kindergarten collaboration, forming a progressive activity sequence from shallow to deep
• Each activity plan follows the five-element structure of "activity objectives - activity preparation - activity process - activity extension - activity evaluation", with the process section including introduction, development, and conclusion stages, specifying specific teacher-child interaction methods
• Apply scaffolding instruction theory to design multi-level support strategies for each activity, including material scaffolding, question scaffolding, peer scaffolding, and teacher scaffolding, meeting learning needs of children at different developmental levels
Deliverable: Series Activity Design Plan Collection (including 8-10 detailed activity plans, activity sequence diagram, layered guidance strategy table) | Quality standard: Complete activity design elements, smooth and layered process, specific and effective support strategies
Step 4
Learning Environment and Learning Center Planning
The core task of this step is to plan the learning environment and learning center settings that align with the theme. Environment is an important educational resource; the Reggio Emilia philosophy refers to environment as the "third teacher". Learning centers are important places for children's autonomous learning and active exploration; the appropriateness of material provision directly affects learning outcomes.

• Following the principles of quiet-active separation, wet-dry separation, and open-closed balance, plan the spatial layout and area proportions of activity zones: art, construction, dramatic play, science exploration, reading, and manipulative/puzzle
• Provide materials in stages according to theme progression, designing the material provision sequence according to "introduction - exploration - deepening - exhibition" phases, embodying hierarchy, openness, and inquiry-based characteristics
• Design interactive walls such as theme wall, question wall, and work display wall, planning ways for children to participate in environment creation, making the environment a carrier for recording and displaying children's learning process
Deliverable: Environment Creation and Learning Center Plan (including space layout diagram, center materials list, theme wall design, staged material provision table) | Quality standard: Scientific and reasonable spatial layout, layered material provision, interactive and educational environment
Step 5
Systematic Curriculum Evaluation Plan Design
The core task of this step is to design a curriculum evaluation plan that combines process-oriented and outcome-oriented assessment. Evaluation is not the endpoint of curriculum but an important means to promote continuous curriculum improvement and children's sustained development. Early childhood education evaluation should follow process-oriented, developmental, and pluralistic principles, focusing on children's performance and progress in the learning process.

• Construct a diversified evaluation system, comprehensively using observation, portfolio analysis, work sampling, conversation, and parent evaluation methods to collect evaluation information from multiple angles
• Design child development evaluation indicators corresponding to curriculum goals, formulating specific and observable evaluation criteria and level descriptions from five domains: health, language, social, science, and art
• Design evaluation plan for the curriculum itself, including four dimensions: goal achievement, activity suitability, environmental effectiveness, and parent satisfaction, providing basis for curriculum improvement
Deliverable: Curriculum Evaluation Plan (including child development evaluation indicators, evaluation toolkit, curriculum quality evaluation form, evaluation implementation guide) | Quality standard: Diversified and comprehensive evaluation system, specific and operable tools, embodying developmental evaluation philosophy
