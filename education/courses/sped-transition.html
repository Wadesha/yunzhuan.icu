转衔教育

Transition Education

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：特殊教育导论、特殊教育评估 | 学期：秋季

本课程聚焦特殊需要学生从学校到成人生活的转衔教育理论与实践。课程内容包括转衔的概念与意义、转衔服务的法律框架与政策要求、个别化转衔计划（ITP）的制定、转衔评估的方法与工具、职业教育与就业准备、独立生活技能培养、社区参与与社交技能发展、高等教育机会的获取、以及家庭与社区资源的链接。学生将学习如何与学生、家庭、雇主及社区机构协作，为特殊需要学生的中学后生活做好充分准备，促进其成功融入社会。

This course focuses on the theory and practice of transition education for students with special needs moving from school to adult life. Topics include transition concepts and significance, legal frameworks and policy requirements, Individualized Transition Plans (ITP), transition assessment methods and tools, vocational education and employment preparation, independent living skills, community participation and social skills development, postsecondary education access, and family and community resource linkage. Students learn to collaborate with students, families, employers, and community agencies to prepare students for post-school success and social inclusion.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：残疾学生个别化转衔计划（ITP）设计

针对一名残疾学生案例，设计从学校到成人生活的个别化转衔计划（ITP），涵盖职业教育、就业、独立生活、社区参与等目标。

For a student with disabilities, design an Individualized Transition Plan (ITP) from school to adult life, covering vocational education, employment, independent living, and community participation goals.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

步骤 1
转衔需求评估与转衔规划
本步骤的核心任务是系统评估特殊学生的转衔需求，制定个别化的转衔计划。转衔（transition）是特殊教育的重要组成部分，帮助特殊学生顺利从一个教育阶段过渡到下一个阶段，从学校过渡到成人生活。转衔规划的核心是以学生为中心，基于学生的兴趣、优势和需求，设计个性化的转衔路径。

• 采用多维度转衔评估工具，从学业能力、职业能力、独立生活能力、社会适应能力、自我决定能力、支持需求等维度全面评估学生的转衔准备度
• 运用以学生为中心的规划方法（Person-Centered Planning），让学生本人、家长、教师、雇主、社区人员等多方参与，共同制定转衔愿景和目标
• 设计个别化转衔计划（ITP），明确转衔目标（就业、继续教育、独立生活、社区参与）、支持服务、负责人员、时间节点和评估标准，确保转衔工作有序推进
产出：转衔需求评估与个别化转衔计划（含评估报告、转衔愿景、ITP计划）｜ 质量标准：评估全面，学生中心，计划具体可操作
步骤 2
转衔课程与职业教育设计
本步骤的核心任务是设计转衔教育课程和职业教育方案，为学生的转衔做好知识技能准备。转衔课程是转衔服务的核心内容，旨在培养学生独立生活、就业和社区参与所需的各种能力。工作本位学习（Work-Based Learning）是职业转衔最有效的方式之一。

• 设计转衔课程体系，包括独立生活课程（个人管理、家庭生活、财务管理、健康管理）、职业课程（职业探索、职业技能、职业素养）、社会参与课程（社区资源利用、社交技能、公民责任）等模块
• 运用生活技能课程（Functional Curriculum）和社区本位教学（Community-Based Instruction），在真实的生活和工作情境中教授实用技能，确保技能的泛化和维持
• 开发职业教育和培训项目，与当地企业、职业学校、社区机构合作，设计实习实训、工学结合、支持性就业等多种职业准备路径
产出：转衔课程与职业教育方案（含课程体系、教学策略、职业项目）｜ 质量标准：课程实用有针对性，教学情境真实，职业路径可行
步骤 3
支持性就业与独立生活支持体系
本步骤的核心任务是构建支持性就业体系和独立生活支持网络，帮助学生实现成功的社区融合。支持性就业（Supported Employment）是国际公认的最有效的残疾人就业模式，强调"先安置后培训"，在真实工作环境中提供持续的支持。独立生活同样需要系统的社区支持服务。

• 建立支持性就业服务体系，包括职业评估、岗位开发、岗位匹配、在岗培训、就业教练、自然支持开发、后续跟进等完整的就业服务链条
• 开发独立生活支持服务，包括住房支持、个人助理服务、财务管理支持、交通支持、医疗保健支持、社交娱乐支持等，满足不同学生的多样化需求
• 构建社区支持网络，整合残疾人服务机构、社区组织、志愿者、企业、家庭等多方资源，形成全方位的社区支持系统
产出：支持性就业与独立生活支持方案（含就业服务体系、生活支持、社区网络）｜ 质量标准：体系完整，服务专业，资源整合充分
步骤 4
转衔服务的跨机构协作机制
本步骤的核心任务是建立跨机构、跨部门的转衔协作机制，形成转衔服务合力。转衔服务涉及教育、人社、民政、残联、卫生健康等多个部门和学校、企业、社区、家庭等多方主体，跨机构协作是转衔服务有效运行的关键。

• 建立转衔协调机构和工作机制，明确各部门、各机构在转衔服务中的职责分工，建立定期会商、信息共享、联合服务的工作制度
• 设计转衔服务的工作流程，从转衔评估、转衔规划、服务提供到效果追踪，明确各环节的衔接机制和责任主体，确保服务的连续性
• 开发统一的转衔信息管理系统，实现教育、就业、社会保障、社区服务等信息的互联互通，为转衔服务提供数据支撑
产出：跨机构转衔协作机制方案（含组织架构、工作流程、信息平台）｜ 质量标准：职责清晰，流程顺畅，机制可持续
步骤 5
转衔效果评价与质量提升
本步骤的核心任务是建立转衔服务质量评价体系，持续提升转衔服务质量。转衔服务的效果最终要体现在学生的成人生活质量上。评价不仅要看就业率、升学率等量化指标，更要看学生的生活满意度、社会参与度和自我决定能力。

• 构建转衔服务质量评价指标体系，包括学生结果指标（就业情况、教育进展、独立生活水平、社会参与、生活质量）、服务过程指标（评估覆盖率、ITP制定率、服务满意度）、系统支持指标（政策保障、经费投入、队伍建设）
• 设计纵向追踪评价机制，对毕业生进行毕业后1年、3年、5年的追踪研究，了解长期转衔效果，为改进服务提供依据
• 建立质量改进机制，定期开展转衔服务质量评估，识别薄弱环节，制定改进措施，推动转衔服务质量的持续提升
产出：转衔服务质量评价与改进方案（含评价指标、追踪方案、改进机制）｜ 质量标准：评价体系科学，追踪设计严谨，改进机制有效

Steps

Step 1
Transition Needs Assessment and Transition Planning
The core task of this step is to systematically assess the transition needs of special needs students and develop individualized transition plans. Transition is an important component of special education, helping special needs students smoothly transition from one educational stage to the next, and from school to adult life. The core of transition planning is student-centered, designing personalized transition paths based on students' interests, strengths, and needs.

• Use multi-dimensional transition assessment tools to comprehensively assess students' transition readiness from dimensions such as academic ability, vocational ability, independent living skills, social adaptation ability, self-determination ability, and support needs
• Apply Person-Centered Planning methods, involving students themselves, parents, teachers, employers, community members and other stakeholders to jointly develop transition visions and goals
• Design Individualized Transition Plans (ITP), clarifying transition goals (employment, continuing education, independent living, community participation), support services, responsible personnel, timelines, and evaluation standards, ensuring orderly progress of transition work
Deliverable: Transition Needs Assessment and Individualized Transition Plan (including assessment report, transition vision, ITP plan) | Quality standard: Comprehensive assessment, student-centered, specific and operable plan
Step 2
Transition Curriculum and Vocational Education Design
The core task of this step is to design transition education curricula and vocational education programs to prepare students with knowledge and skills for transition. Transition curriculum is the core content of transition services, aiming to cultivate various abilities students need for independent living, employment, and community participation. Work-Based Learning is one of the most effective methods for vocational transition.

• Design a transition curriculum system, including modules such as independent living curriculum (personal management, family life, financial management, health management), vocational curriculum (career exploration, vocational skills, vocational literacy), social participation curriculum (community resource utilization, social skills, civic responsibility)
• Apply Functional Curriculum and Community-Based Instruction, teaching practical skills in real life and work contexts, ensuring generalization and maintenance of skills
• Develop vocational education and training programs, collaborating with local enterprises, vocational schools, and community organizations to design multiple vocational preparation paths such as internships, work-study programs, and supported employment
Deliverable: Transition Curriculum and Vocational Education Plan (including curriculum system, teaching strategies, vocational programs) | Quality standard: Practical and targeted curriculum, authentic teaching contexts, feasible vocational paths
Step 3
Supported Employment and Independent Living Support System
The core task of this step is to construct a supported employment system and independent living support network to help students achieve successful community integration. Supported Employment is an internationally recognized most effective employment model for people with disabilities, emphasizing "place then train" and providing ongoing support in real work environments. Independent living also requires systematic community support services.

• Establish a supported employment service system, including a complete employment service chain of vocational assessment, job development, job matching, on-the-job training, job coaching, natural support development, and follow-up
• Develop independent living support services, including housing support, personal assistant services, financial management support, transportation support, healthcare support, social and recreational support, etc., meeting the diverse needs of different students
• Build a community support network, integrating multiple resources such as disability service agencies, community organizations, volunteers, enterprises, and families to form a comprehensive community support system
Deliverable: Supported Employment and Independent Living Support Plan (including employment service system, living support, community network) | Quality standard: Complete system, professional services, sufficient resource integration
Step 4
Inter-agency Collaboration Mechanism for Transition Services
The core task of this step is to establish inter-agency and inter-departmental transition collaboration mechanisms to form synergy in transition services. Transition services involve multiple departments such as education, human resources, civil affairs, disabled persons' federations, health, and multiple entities including schools, enterprises, communities, and families. Inter-agency collaboration is the key to effective operation of transition services.

• Establish transition coordination机构 and working mechanisms, clarify the responsibilities of each department and agency in transition services, and establish work systems for regular consultation, information sharing, and joint services
• Design work processes for transition services, from transition assessment, transition planning, service provision to effect tracking, clarify the connection mechanisms and responsible entities of each link, ensuring service continuity
• Develop a unified transition information management system to realize interconnection of education, employment, social security, community service and other information, providing data support for transition services
Deliverable: Inter-agency Transition Collaboration Mechanism Plan (including organizational structure, work processes, information platform) | Quality standard: Clear responsibilities, smooth processes, sustainable mechanism
Step 5
Transition Effect Evaluation and Quality Improvement
The core task of this step is to establish a transition service quality evaluation system and continuously improve transition service quality. The effectiveness of transition services is ultimately reflected in students' adult quality of life. Evaluation should not only look at quantitative indicators such as employment rate and enrollment rate, but more importantly at students' life satisfaction, social participation, and self-determination ability.

• Construct a transition service quality evaluation indicator system, including student outcome indicators (employment situation, educational progress, independent living level, social participation, quality of life), service process indicators (assessment coverage rate, ITP development rate, service satisfaction), system support indicators (policy guarantee, funding input, team building)
• Design longitudinal tracking evaluation mechanisms to conduct follow-up studies on graduates 1 year, 3 years, and 5 years after graduation, understanding long-term transition effects and providing basis for service improvement
• Establish a quality improvement mechanism, regularly conduct transition service quality assessment, identify weak links, develop improvement measures, and promote continuous improvement of transition service quality
Deliverable: Transition Service Quality Evaluation and Improvement Plan (including evaluation indicators, tracking plan, improvement mechanism) | Quality standard: Scientific evaluation system, rigorous tracking design, effective improvement mechanism
