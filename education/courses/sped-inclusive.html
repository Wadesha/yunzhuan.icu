全纳教育

Inclusive Education

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：特殊教育导论 | 学期：春季

本课程探讨全纳教育的理论基础、国际发展趋势与实践策略。课程内容涵盖全纳教育的概念与价值观、从隔离到融合的历史演变、全纳教育的法律与政策支持、普通学校的全纳教育实践模式、合作教学与团队协作、通用学习设计（UDL）、差异化教学、同伴支持策略、以及学校文化与氛围的营造。学生将了解不同国家和地区的全纳教育经验，分析全纳教育实施中的挑战与对策，树立尊重差异、全员参与的教育理念。

This course explores the theoretical foundations, international development, and practical strategies of inclusive education. Topics include concepts and values of inclusion, historical evolution from segregation to integration, legal and policy support, inclusive school practice models, co-teaching and team collaboration, Universal Design for Learning (UDL), differentiated instruction, peer support strategies, and school culture development. Students examine international experiences, analyze implementation challenges, and develop values centered on respect for diversity and full participation.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：全纳课堂通用学习设计调整方案

选择某节普通课程，运用通用学习设计（UDL）和差异化教学原则，为班内特殊需要学生设计课堂调整方案，含教学目标、方法、评价三层面的调整。

Select a general education lesson, apply Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and differentiated instruction principles to design classroom adaptations for students with special needs, covering objectives, methods, and assessment.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

📋 示例：为一名视障学生随班就读的五年级班级设计融合支持方案。你需要评估学生的视力状况和学习需求，改造教室环境（如调整座位、提供放大设备和助视器），与学科教师协作调整教学材料（如大字版教材、触觉图形、音频资源），培训班级同学做同伴支持伙伴，设计个别辅导计划并建立定期沟通协调机制。
步骤 1
融合教育现状调研与需求评估
本步骤的核心任务是调研学校融合教育的现状，系统评估特殊教育需要学生的需求和学校的支持能力。融合教育不是简单地把特殊儿童放进普通教室，而是需要系统的支持体系。全面的现状调研和需求评估是有效推进融合教育的前提。

• 采用问卷调查、访谈、课堂观察等方法，从学校管理、教师素养、课程教学、支持服务、同伴关系、家校合作六个维度评估学校融合教育的现状和存在的问题
• 对有特殊教育需要的学生进行全面评估，包括障碍类型与程度、学业水平、社交能力、适应行为、支持需求等，建立学生特殊教育档案
• 评估普通教师的融合教育素养和专业发展需求，识别教师在知识、技能、态度、信心等方面的差距，为专业发展提供依据
产出：融合教育现状与需求评估报告（含学校现状、学生需求、教师需求）｜ 质量标准：调研全面深入，需求评估精准，数据支撑充分
步骤 2
融合教育支持体系构建
本步骤的核心任务是构建多层次、全方位的融合教育支持体系。融合教育的质量取决于支持体系的完善程度。借鉴反应干预（RTI）和多层级支持系统（MTSS）的理念，构建全员、全程、全方位的支持网络。

• 构建三级支持体系：一级为全员性支持（通用课程调整、差异化教学、积极行为支持），二级为靶向性支持（小组辅导、资源教室、补救教学），三级为个别化支持（IEP、专项康复、个别辅导）
• 建立融合教育专业支持团队，包括资源教师、巡回辅导教师、特殊教育专家、康复治疗师、心理辅导师等，明确职责分工和协作机制
• 完善融合教育的制度保障，包括学生筛查转介制度、个别化教育制度、资源教室管理制度、教师培训制度、经费保障制度等
产出：融合教育支持体系方案（含三级支持模型、团队建设、制度保障）｜ 质量标准：体系完整有层次，团队配置合理，制度健全可行
步骤 3
课程调整与差异化教学设计
本步骤的核心任务是设计适合所有学生的课程和差异化教学方案。融合教育的核心是课程和教学的调整，让每个学生都能在原有基础上获得发展。通用设计学习（UDL）和差异化教学（DI）是融合课堂的核心教学策略。

• 依据通用设计学习（UDL）的三大原则（多元表征、多元行动与表达、多元投入）调整课程设计，从设计之初就考虑多样性需求，减少后续的个别调整
• 设计差异化教学方案，在内容（学什么）、过程（怎么学）、成果（怎么展示）、环境（在哪里学）四个维度进行差异化设计，满足不同学生的学习需要
• 开发课程调整的分层策略，包括调整课程内容的深度和广度、调整呈现方式、调整学习任务、调整评价方式等，形成课程调整工具包
产出：融合课程与差异化教学方案（含UDL课程设计、差异化策略、调整工具包）｜ 质量标准：体现UDL理念，差异化策略具体可操作，工具包实用
步骤 4
融合班级环境建设与同伴支持
本步骤的核心任务是创设包容、接纳、支持的融合班级环境，建立同伴支持系统。心理环境和同伴关系是影响融合教育质量的关键因素之一，有时比物理环境和教学调整更重要。积极的同伴关系不仅能促进特殊学生的发展，也能促进所有学生的社会性发展。

• 营造包容接纳的班级文化，通过全班教学、主题班会、绘本阅读、集体活动等方式，开展多样性教育和同理心教育，减少偏见和歧视
• 建立同伴支持系统，如同伴辅导、同伴调解、合作学习小组、结对伙伴等，让普通学生成为融合教育的积极参与者和支持者
• 优化班级物理环境，进行无障碍环境改造，创设多元化的学习角和活动区，提供支持性的学习材料和辅助技术，让每个学生都能参与和成功
产出：融合班级环境建设方案（含班级文化建设、同伴支持系统、物理环境优化）｜ 质量标准：班级文化有温度，同伴支持有实效，环境友好无障碍
步骤 5
融合教育质量评价与持续改进
本步骤的核心任务是建立融合教育质量评价体系，形成持续改进机制。融合教育的质量不能只用学业成绩衡量，更要关注学生的归属感、参与度、社会性发展和生活质量。发展性评价和过程性评价是融合教育评价的基本原则。

• 构建多元化的融合教育质量评价指标体系，包括学生发展（学业、社交、情绪、适应）、教学质量（课程调整、差异化教学、参与度）、支持系统（资源配置、专业支持、家校合作）、学校文化（包容氛围、领导力、教师专业发展）四个维度
• 设计多元评价方法，综合运用标准化测验、课堂观察、访谈、档案袋评价、学生满意度调查、家长满意度调查等，全面评价融合教育质量
• 建立持续改进机制，定期开展融合教育质量评估，识别优势和不足，制定改进目标和措施，形成"评估-反馈-改进-再评估"的良性循环
产出：融合教育质量评价与改进方案（含评价指标、评价工具、改进机制）｜ 质量标准：评价体系科学多元，工具适切有效，改进机制持续运行

Steps

Step 1
Inclusive Education Status Survey and Needs Assessment
The core task of this step is to investigate the current status of inclusive education in schools and systematically assess the needs of students with special educational needs and the support capacity of schools. Inclusive education is not simply placing children with special needs in regular classrooms but requires a systematic support system. Comprehensive status investigation and needs assessment are prerequisites for effectively promoting inclusive education.

• Use questionnaire surveys, interviews, classroom observation methods to assess the current status and existing problems of school inclusive education from six dimensions: school management, teacher quality, curriculum and teaching, support services, peer relationships, and home-school collaboration
• Conduct comprehensive assessment of students with special educational needs, including disability type and degree, academic level, social competence, adaptive behavior, support needs, etc., and establish special education files for students
• Assess the inclusive education literacy and professional development needs of general teachers, identify gaps in teachers' knowledge, skills, attitudes, confidence, etc., providing basis for professional development
Deliverable: Inclusive Education Status and Needs Assessment Report (including school status, student needs, teacher needs) | Quality standard: Comprehensive and in-depth investigation, accurate needs assessment, sufficient data support
Step 2
Inclusive Education Support System Construction
The core task of this step is to construct a multi-level, comprehensive inclusive education support system. The quality of inclusive education depends on the perfection of the support system. Drawing on the concepts of Response to Intervention (RTI) and Multi-Tiered System of Supports (MTSS), build a support network for all students, throughout the process, and in all aspects.

• Construct a three-tier support system: Tier 1 is universal support (universal curriculum adaptation, differentiated instruction, positive behavior support), Tier 2 is targeted support (small group tutoring, resource room, remedial teaching), Tier 3 is individualized support (IEP, specialized rehabilitation, individual tutoring)
• Establish an inclusive education professional support team, including resource teachers, itinerant support teachers, special education experts, rehabilitation therapists, psychological counselors, etc., clarify responsibility division and collaboration mechanism
• Improve the institutional guarantee for inclusive education, including student screening and referral system, individualized education system, resource room management system, teacher training system, funding guarantee system, etc.
Deliverable: Inclusive Education Support System Plan (including three-tier support model, team building, institutional guarantee) | Quality standard: Complete and hierarchical system, reasonable team configuration, sound and feasible institutions
Step 3
Curriculum Adaptation and Differentiated Teaching Design
The core task of this step is to design curricula and differentiated teaching plans suitable for all students. The core of inclusive education is curriculum and teaching adaptation, enabling every student to develop on their existing basis. Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and Differentiated Instruction (DI) are core teaching strategies for inclusive classrooms.

• Adapt curriculum design according to the three principles of Universal Design for Learning (multiple representation, multiple action and expression, multiple engagement), considering diversity needs from the beginning of design to reduce subsequent individual adjustments
• Design differentiated teaching plans, conduct differentiated design in four dimensions: content (what to learn), process (how to learn), product (how to demonstrate), environment (where to learn), meeting different students' learning needs
• Develop layered strategies for curriculum adaptation, including adjusting depth and breadth of curriculum content, adjusting presentation methods, adjusting learning tasks, adjusting evaluation methods, etc., forming a curriculum adaptation toolkit
Deliverable: Inclusive Curriculum and Differentiated Teaching Plan (including UDL curriculum design, differentiated strategies, adaptation toolkit) | Quality standard: Embodying UDL philosophy, specific and operable differentiated strategies, practical toolkit
Step 4
Inclusive Classroom Environment Building and Peer Support
The core task of this step is to create an inclusive, accepting, and supportive inclusive classroom environment and establish a peer support system. Psychological environment and peer relationships are among the key factors affecting the quality of inclusive education, sometimes more important than physical environment and teaching adaptation. Positive peer relationships not only promote the development of special needs students but also promote the social development of all students.

• Build an inclusive and accepting classroom culture, carry out diversity education and empathy education through whole-class teaching, themed class meetings, picture book reading, collective activities, etc., reducing prejudice and discrimination
• Establish peer support systems, such as peer tutoring, peer mediation, cooperative learning groups, buddy systems, etc., making general students active participants and supporters of inclusive education
• Optimize the physical environment of the classroom, conduct barrier-free environmental改造, create diverse learning corners and activity areas, provide supportive learning materials and assistive technology, enabling every student to participate and succeed
Deliverable: Inclusive Classroom Environment Building Plan (including classroom culture building, peer support system, physical environment optimization) | Quality standard: Warm classroom culture, effective peer support, friendly and barrier-free environment
Step 5
Inclusive Education Quality Evaluation and Continuous Improvement
The core task of this step is to establish an inclusive education quality evaluation system and form a continuous improvement mechanism. The quality of inclusive education cannot be measured solely by academic achievement; more attention should be paid to students' sense of belonging, participation, social development, and quality of life. Developmental evaluation and process evaluation are the basic principles of inclusive education evaluation.

• Construct a diversified inclusive education quality evaluation indicator system, including four dimensions: student development (academics, social, emotional, adaptation), teaching quality (curriculum adaptation, differentiated instruction, participation), support system (resource allocation, professional support, home-school collaboration), school culture (inclusive atmosphere, leadership, teacher professional development)
• Design multiple evaluation methods, comprehensively using standardized tests, classroom observation, interviews, portfolio assessment, student satisfaction surveys, parent satisfaction surveys, etc., to comprehensively evaluate inclusive education quality
• Establish a continuous improvement mechanism, regularly conduct inclusive education quality assessment, identify strengths and weaknesses, develop improvement goals and measures, forming a virtuous cycle of "assessment-feedback-improvement-reassessment"
Deliverable: Inclusive Education Quality Evaluation and Improvement Plan (including evaluation indicators, evaluation tools, improvement mechanism) | Quality standard: Scientific and diversified evaluation system, appropriate and effective tools, continuously operating improvement mechanism
