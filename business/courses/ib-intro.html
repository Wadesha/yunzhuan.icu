国际商务导论

Introduction to International Business

课程介绍 Course Introduction

学分：3 | 先修课：无 | 学期：大一春季

本课程是国际商务专业的入门基础课，全面介绍国际商务的基本概念、理论框架与实践领域。课程涵盖全球化与国际商务环境、国际贸易与投资理论、跨国公司战略、国际市场进入模式、跨文化管理等核心内容。通过真实案例分析，学生将理解企业在国际市场上面临的机遇与挑战，培养全球视野与跨文化思维能力，为后续专业课程学习奠定基础。

This introductory course provides a comprehensive overview of fundamental concepts, theoretical frameworks, and practical areas of international business. Topics include globalization and the international business environment, international trade and investment theories, multinational corporation strategy, market entry modes, and cross-cultural management. Through real-world case studies, students develop a global perspective and cross-cultural thinking skills.

大作业 Final Project

作业标题：企业国际化市场进入战略分析

选取一家拟国际化的企业，分析其目标市场的国际商务环境，评估各市场进入模式（出口、许可、合资、独资）的优劣，设计国际化战略方案并提出跨文化管理建议。

Select a company planning to internationalize, analyze its target market's international business environment, evaluate market entry modes (export, licensing, JV, wholly-owned), design an internationalization strategy, and provide cross-cultural recommendations.

实施步骤 Implementation Steps

📋 示例：为一家中国企业制定出海计划，比如一家智能硬件品牌进入东南亚市场。你需要分析目标国的政治、法律和文化环境，评估进入模式（出口、合资、独资），最后制定3年市场进入路线图。
步骤 1
国际环境分析与市场选择
本步骤的核心任务是分析国际商务环境，选择目标市场并评估市场吸引力。包括宏观环境分析（政治、经济、文化、技术、法律、生态）、行业分析、市场潜力评估，为市场进入战略提供依据。

• 进行PESTEL分析：从政治（Political）、经济（Economic）、社会文化（Social）、技术（Technological）、环境（Environmental）、法律（Legal）六个维度分析目标国宏观环境
• 评估市场吸引力：分析市场规模、市场增长率、竞争强度、进入壁垒、利润潜力，使用市场吸引力矩阵进行多维度评估，进行市场潜力测算
• 进行竞争对手分析：识别主要国际竞争对手和本地竞争对手，分析其战略、优势劣势、市场份额，绘制竞争格局图，识别竞争空白点
产出：国际市场分析报告（含PESTEL分析、市场吸引力评估、竞争格局分析、目标市场选择）｜ 质量标准：环境分析全面深入，市场选择有数据支撑，竞争分析有洞察力
步骤 2
进入模式选择与战略制定
本步骤的核心任务是选择合适的国际市场进入模式，制定国际化经营战略。包括出口模式、契约模式（许可经营、特许经营）、投资模式（合资、独资、并购）等进入模式的比较和选择。

• 分析各进入模式的优缺点：比较出口（直接出口/间接出口）、契约进入（许可、特许、交钥匙工程）、投资进入（合资、独资、并购、绿地投资）的优缺点、适用条件和风险
• 选择最优进入模式：基于企业战略目标、资源能力、目标国风险、市场规模等因素，使用决策矩阵或层次分析法（AHP）选择最优进入模式
• 制定国际化战略：确定国际战略类型（国际战略、多国本土化战略、全球战略、跨国战略），制定产品/服务策略、定价策略、营销策略、人力资源策略
产出：进入战略方案（含进入模式比较分析、最优进入模式选择、国际化战略制定）｜ 质量标准：进入模式选择有依据，战略制定符合企业实际和市场特点，可操作性强
步骤 3
跨文化管理与本地化运营
本步骤的核心任务是进行跨文化管理，制定本地化运营策略，确保跨国经营的文化适应性。包括文化差异分析、跨文化沟通策略、本地化战略设计、人力资源跨文化管理。

• 进行文化差异分析：使用霍夫斯泰德（Hofstede）文化维度理论（权力距离、个人主义/集体主义、男性化/女性化、不确定性规避、长期导向、放纵与约束）分析母国与东道国的文化差异
• 制定跨文化管理策略：设计跨文化培训方案，建立跨文化沟通机制，培养文化智力（CQ），制定文化冲突预防和解决机制，管理多元化团队
• 设计本地化战略：产品本地化（适应当地需求和标准）、营销本地化（适应当地语言、文化和消费习惯）、人力资源本地化（本地员工招聘和培养）、供应链本地化
产出：跨文化管理与本地化方案（含文化差异分析、跨文化管理策略、本地化战略设计）｜ 质量标准：文化分析维度完整，跨文化管理策略实用，本地化方案具体可行
步骤 4
国际法律合规与风险管理
本步骤的核心任务是确保国际商务活动的法律合规性，识别和管理国际经营中的各类风险。包括国际贸易法律、投资法律、知识产权保护、外汇风险、政治风险、文化风险等的管理。

• 法律合规审查：研究目标国的外商投资法、公司法、劳动法、税法、知识产权法、反垄断法，进行合规审查，识别法律风险点，制定合规方案和合规管理体系
• 风险管理体系：建立国际经营风险评估框架，识别政治风险、经济风险、外汇风险、法律风险、文化风险、运营风险，评估风险发生概率和影响程度，绘制风险矩阵
• 制定风险应对策略：针对不同类型的风险制定相应的应对策略（风险规避、风险降低、风险转移、风险承受），设计风险监控和预警机制，制定应急预案
产出：法律合规与风险管理报告（含法律合规审查、风险识别与评估、风险应对策略）｜ 质量标准：法律审查专业全面，风险识别完整，应对策略具体有效
步骤 5
实施规划与绩效评估
本步骤的核心任务是制定详细的实施规划，建立绩效评估体系，确保国际化战略的有效执行和持续优化。包括实施路线图、资源配置、组织架构设计、关键绩效指标设定。

• 制定实施路线图：将国际化战略分解为具体的行动步骤，制定时间表和里程碑，明确各阶段的目标、任务和责任人，编制甘特图和实施计划
• 设计组织架构和资源配置：确定国际业务的组织架构（国际事业部、全球产品结构、全球区域结构、矩阵结构），配置人力资源、财务资源和技术资源
• 建立绩效评估体系：设定关键绩效指标（KPI），包括财务指标（ROI、市场份额、收入增长）和非财务指标（客户满意度、员工满意度、品牌认知度），建立定期评估和调整机制
产出：实施规划与绩效体系（含实施路线图、组织架构设计、KPI绩效评估体系）｜ 质量标准：实施规划详细可执行，组织架构合理，绩效评估体系科学

Steps

Step 1
International Environment Analysis & Market Selection
The core task of this step is to analyze the international business environment, select target markets and assess market attractiveness. Includes macro environment analysis (political, economic, cultural, technological, legal, ecological), industry analysis, market potential assessment.

• Conduct PESTEL analysis: analyze target country macro environment from six dimensions: Political, Economic, Social-cultural, Technological, Environmental, Legal
• Assess market attractiveness: analyze market size, market growth rate, competitive intensity, entry barriers, profit potential, use market attractiveness matrix for multi-dimensional evaluation, conduct market potential estimation
• Conduct competitor analysis: identify major international and local competitors, analyze their strategies, strengths and weaknesses, market share, draw competitive landscape map, identify competitive gaps
Deliverable: International market analysis report (including PESTEL analysis, market attractiveness assessment, competitive landscape analysis, target market selection) | Quality standard: Comprehensive in-depth environment analysis, data-supported market selection, insightful competitive analysis
Step 2
Entry Mode Selection & Strategy Formulation
The core task of this step is to select appropriate international market entry modes and develop international business strategy. Includes comparison and selection of export modes, contractual modes (licensing, franchising), investment modes (joint venture, wholly-owned, M&A).

• Analyze advantages and disadvantages of each entry mode: compare export (direct/indirect export), contractual entry (licensing, franchising, turnkey projects), investment entry (joint venture, WOS, M&A, greenfield investment) - their pros, cons, applicable conditions and risks
• Select optimal entry mode: based on enterprise strategic goals, resource capabilities, target country risk, market size and other factors, use decision matrix or analytic hierarchy process (AHP) to select optimal entry mode
• Develop international strategy: determine international strategy type (international strategy, multidomestic strategy, global strategy, transnational strategy), formulate product/service strategy, pricing strategy, marketing strategy, HR strategy
Deliverable: Entry strategy plan (including entry mode comparative analysis, optimal entry mode selection, international strategy formulation) | Quality standard: Evidence-based entry mode selection, strategy matches enterprise reality and market characteristics, strong operability
Step 3
Cross-Cultural Management & Localization
The core task of this step is to conduct cross-cultural management, develop localization operation strategies, and ensure cultural adaptability of international operations. Includes cultural difference analysis, cross-cultural communication strategy, localization strategy design, cross-cultural HR management.

• Conduct cultural difference analysis: use Hofstede's cultural dimensions theory (power distance, individualism/collectivism, masculinity/femininity, uncertainty avoidance, long-term orientation, indulgence vs restraint) to analyze cultural differences between home and host countries
• Develop cross-cultural management strategy: design cross-cultural training programs, establish cross-cultural communication mechanisms, develop cultural intelligence (CQ), formulate cultural conflict prevention and resolution mechanisms, manage diverse teams
• Design localization strategy: product localization (adapt to local needs and standards), marketing localization (adapt to local language, culture and consumption habits), HR localization (local employee recruitment and development), supply chain localization
Deliverable: Cross-cultural management and localization plan (including cultural difference analysis, cross-cultural management strategy, localization strategy design) | Quality standard: Complete cultural analysis dimensions, practical cross-cultural management strategies, specific feasible localization plans
Step 4
International Legal Compliance & Risk Management
The core task of this step is to ensure legal compliance of international business activities and identify/manage various risks in international operations. Includes international trade law, investment law, IP protection, foreign exchange risk, political risk, cultural risk management.

• Legal compliance review: research target country's foreign investment law, company law, labor law, tax law, IP law, antitrust law, conduct compliance review, identify legal risk points, develop compliance plans and compliance management systems
• Risk management system: establish international operation risk assessment framework, identify political, economic, foreign exchange, legal, cultural, operational risks, assess probability and impact, draw risk matrix
• Develop risk response strategies: formulate corresponding response strategies for different types of risks (risk avoidance, risk reduction, risk transfer, risk acceptance), design risk monitoring and early warning mechanisms, develop contingency plans
Deliverable: Legal compliance and risk management report (including legal compliance review, risk identification and assessment, risk response strategies) | Quality standard: Professional comprehensive legal review, complete risk identification, specific effective response strategies
Step 5
Implementation Planning & Performance Evaluation
The core task of this step is to develop detailed implementation plans and establish performance evaluation systems to ensure effective execution and continuous optimization of international strategies. Includes implementation roadmap, resource allocation, organizational structure design, KPI setting.

• Develop implementation roadmap: decompose international strategy into specific action steps, create timeline and milestones, clarify goals, tasks and responsible persons for each phase, prepare Gantt chart and implementation plan
• Design organizational structure and resource allocation: determine international business organizational structure (international division, global product structure, global geographic structure, matrix structure), allocate human, financial and technological resources
• Establish performance evaluation system: set key performance indicators (KPIs), including financial indicators (ROI, market share, revenue growth) and non-financial indicators (customer satisfaction, employee satisfaction, brand awareness), establish regular evaluation and adjustment mechanisms
Deliverable: Implementation plan and performance system (including implementation roadmap, organizational structure design, KPI performance evaluation system) | Quality standard: Detailed executable implementation plan, reasonable organizational structure, scientific performance evaluation system
